# Theological brief — MAT-028: Judging Others (MAT.7.1-6)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus directs His disciples how to conduct themselves toward the faults of others. He forbids censorious, magisterial judging of a brother (v1), warns that the standard and measure we apply will be applied back to us (v2), and exposes the absurdity of straining to remove a speck from a brother's eye while a beam blinds our own (v3-4). The remedy is order, not silence: "first cast out the beam out of thine own eye," and *then* see clearly to help the brother (v5). Finally, reproof itself must discriminate — holy things are not to be forced on the obstinately profane (v6).

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Judge* — not the magistrate's lawful office but private, rash, uncharitable censure (MHC). *Mote* — a splinter, any small fault; *beam* — the far greater fault we overlook in ourselves (JFB). *Hypocrite* — one who pretends zealous, compassionate charity while leaving worse faults uncorrected in himself; he alone is fit to reprove who severely judges himself (JFB). *Dogs and swine* — the notoriously, obstinately vile who hate reproof and rend the reprover; to be named such only "after trial, and upon full evidence" (MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive this text as God's inspired, sufficient Word, its sense searched by clearer places (1.9). Doctrine: WLC Q145 (via v1) numbers "rash, harsh, and partial censuring" and "misconstructing intentions, words, and actions" among sins forbidden in the ninth commandment. On v6, the confession draws only the principle that holy things must not be profaned upon the obstinate; the reproof here is private, not formal church censure.

ROM.14.10-13 — do not judge a fellow servant. 1CO.4.3-5 — leave final judgment to the Lord. LUK.6.41 — the speck-and-beam parallel. GAL.6.1 — restore gently, watching yourself. JHN.8.7-9 — the sinless-first principle. MAT.7.5 — self-cleansing precedes helping. PRO.9.7-8 / PRO.23.9 / PRO.26.11 — reproving the scorner rebounds. MAT.10.14-15 / MAT.15.26 — holy things withheld from those who refuse them.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** Teaching sayings carried by vivid figures. D7 = image -> referent (speck/beam, dogs/swine/pearls); mark the pivot at v6, where the passage turns from the sin of over-judging to the opposite error of undiscriminating reproof. D3 = fix the sense of *mote*, *beam*, *hypocrite*, *dogs/swine* as the commentary gives them.

Off-agenda (set aside): the Lord's-Supper/fencing-the-table content of WCF 29.8, 30.3, and WLC Q173 (proof-texted via v6) — used only for their reading of "holy things," not imported as this passage's subject.
