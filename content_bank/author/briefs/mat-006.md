# Theological Brief — MAT-006: The Return to Nazareth (Matthew 2:19–23)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** This passage narrates God's providential guidance of the holy family out of Egypt back to the land of Israel after Herod's death. The stress falls on divine direction given by degrees: an angel commands the return, then a further dream redirects Joseph away from Judea (where Archelaus now reigns) to Galilee, settling in Nazareth. Joseph obeys immediately at each step. The movement culminates in fulfillment — "He will be called a Nazarene" — so that even the family's humble resettlement answers what "was spoken through the prophets."

**Key terms (from commentary).** *"They are dead which sought the young Child's life"* — a plural for Herod, the persecutor, whom the child outlives (JFB notes the echo of Exodus 4:19). *"Nazarene"* — carrying, per the commentary, both a note of contempt ("Can any good thing come out of Nazareth?") and, by allusion to *netzer* (Isaiah 11:1), the lowly Branch springing from Jesse's stump; MHC adds the sense of one set apart. *"Land of Israel"* — the general region, the precise place withheld until further guidance.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read under WCF 1, this text is received as inspired and God-breathed. WCF 1.9 governs the method: the "spoken through the prophets" citation is searched by the clearer places (Isaiah 11:1; the Nazarite figures), Scripture interpreting Scripture. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrinal claim is asserted beyond the passage's own testimony to God's guiding word and providence.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** Standard narrative; D7 = follow the sequence of dreams and Joseph's obedience. Note one shift at v23: the plain travel account turns to a fulfillment formula, so D7 there = citation → prophetic referent (the *netzer* / Nazarite allusion), and D3 on "Nazarene" (contempt and lowly-Branch senses).
