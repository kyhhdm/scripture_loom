# Theological Brief — ECC-008: Reverence in Worship and Vows (ECC 5:1-8)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** The Preacher redirects the reader from the world's vanity to the house of God, and there presses one demand: reverence. Guard your steps; draw near to *listen* rather than offer the sacrifice of fools. Because God is in heaven and you are on earth, let your words be few — hasty speech in prayer is a fool's mark. The same reverence governs vows: pay what you vow, promptly and in full; better not to vow than to vow and fail. Do not excuse a broken vow before the messenger, lest God be angry. The passage closes: *fear God* — and, so fearing, be not astonished at oppression, for higher ones watch over the high.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Guard your steps" — approach worship with solemn, composed seriousness, thoughts and affections gathered (MHC). "Sacrifice of fools" — outward, hypocritical performance resting in the sign, which God abhors. "Let your words be few" — reverent, deliberate speech, not careless repetition; the fool trusts to be heard for much speaking (JFB, Matt 6:7). "Vow" — a bond upon the soul (Num 30:2) that must be paid in kind, not altered. "Fear God" — the remedy for both empty worship and the fear of man.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Reading the text as inspired, sufficient, and self-interpreting (WCF 1): WCF 21.3 (via 5:1-2) — prayer must be made with understanding, reverence, humility, faith. WCF 21.5 (via 5:4-5) and 22.5 (via 5:4-6) — vows are true worship, made and performed with religious care and faithfulness. WLC Q112 (via 5:1) — God's ordinances, prayer, and vows are to be holily and reverently used. WLC Q185 (via 5:1) — pray with awful apprehension of God's majesty and a deep sense of our unworthiness.

**Cross-references.** MAT.6.7 — vain repetition condemned (5:2). JAS.1.19 — swift to hear, slow to speak (5:1). PRO.10.19 — many words, much sin (5:2). ISA.55.9 — God's heaven above earth (5:2). NUM.30.2 / DEU.23.21-23 — the binding, prompt payment of vows (5:4). HEB.12.28-29 — reverent, godly-fearing worship (5:1). MAT.12.36 — account for idle words (5:7). PRO.15.8 — the wicked's sacrifice abhorred (5:1). ECC.12.13 — fear God, the book's summary (5:7).

**Reading moves.** Wisdom-genre exhortation. D7 = trace the repeated imperatives to their single root, "fear God" (v7), which governs both the worship section (vv1-3) and the vow section (vv4-6); note the proverb-figure at v3/v7 (dreams : many cares :: fool's speech : many words) — image serving argument, not standalone metaphor. D3 = weigh the passage's charged terms — *guard*, *listen*, *few*, *vow*, *fear*.

Set aside as off-agenda: WLC Q151's catalogue of sin-aggravations and Q175 on post-Supper duty are cited only for their touching on vows; they contribute nothing here beyond affirming that vows bind — no Lord's-Supper or sin-gradation doctrine is in this passage's plain sense.
