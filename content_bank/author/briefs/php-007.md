# PHP-007 — True Righteousness Through Christ, Not the Law (PHP.3.1–14)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Paul, warning against the judaizing "dogs," sets two grounds of confidence side by side and renounces one for the other. He inventories his own flesh-credentials — circumcised the eighth day, tribe of Benjamin, a Pharisee, "as to righteousness in the law, faultless" — then counts them all loss, indeed rubbish, "for the surpassing excellence of knowing Christ Jesus." His aim is to be *found in Him*, "not having my own righteousness from the law, but that which is through faith in Christ." Yet he insists he has not already attained or been made perfect; he presses on toward the goal, since Christ first took hold of him.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Confidence in the flesh* — trusting outward status and law-keeping (MHC). *Concision* — the legalists' mere flesh-cutting, having lost circumcision's spiritual sense (JFB). *Rubbish/dung* — refuse cast to dogs, contemptible beside Christ (JFB). *Found in Him* — safe as the manslayer in the city of refuge, appearing before the Judge clothed in a complete righteousness (MHC). *Press on / reaching forth* — the runner straining toward the mark (MHC, JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Reading the text as inspired and sufficient, Scripture interpreting Scripture: v.9 grounds *justification* — God accepts sinners as righteous not for anything wrought in them but by imputing Christ's obedience, received by faith, itself God's gift (WCF 11.1; WLC Q70, Q72, Q73). Verse 10 grounds *sanctification* through the virtue of Christ's death and resurrection (WCF 13.1) and *communion* with Him in His sufferings (WCF 26.1). Verse 12 anchors sanctification's real-but-imperfect struggle in this life (WCF 13.2; WLC Q77 — justification imputes, sanctification infuses and subdues, "neither equal in all, nor in this life perfect in any").

**Cross-references.** PHP.3.14→1CO.9.24 (run to win the prize); PHP.3.13→LUK.9.62 (no looking back); PHP.3.10→GAL.2.20 (crucified with Christ, He lives in me); PHP.3.13→HEB.12.1–2 (lay aside weights, run the race); PHP.3.14→2TI.4.7–8 (finished the race, the crown); PHP.3.8→JER.9.23–24 (glory not in self but in knowing the Lord).

**Reading moves.** Epistle: D7 = trace the argument — Paul's "but whatever was gain… I count as loss" turns the credential-list (vv.5–6) into its opposite; watch the pivot at v.7 and the "not… but" of v.9. D3 = the load-bearing contrast terms *gain/loss*, *own righteousness / righteousness from God*, *flesh / faith*.

Set aside as off-agenda: WLC Q60 (fate of those never hearing the gospel) and Q172 (doubting communicants at the Lord's Supper) — used here only for their affirmation that there is salvation in Christ alone and the desire "to be found in Christ."
