# Theological Brief — PHP-008: Following Paul's Example, Citizenship in Heaven (PHP 3:15-21)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Paul urges the mature to hold the mind he has just described (3:7-14) and to keep marching by what they have already attained (v.16). He offers himself and those who walk by the same pattern as examples to imitate (v.17), then sets against them, with tears, the "many" who live as enemies of the cross — whose god is their belly, whose glory is their shame, who mind earthly things, and whose end is destruction (vv.18-19). The contrast turns on citizenship: ours is in heaven, from which we eagerly await the Savior, the Lord Jesus Christ (v.20), who by his subduing power will transform our lowly bodies to be like his glorious body (v.21).

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Mature/perfect" — full-grown, fit for running the course, not yet crowned with complete victory (JFB). "Enemies of the cross" — such in practice, not doctrine; known by their walk, not their profession (JFB, MHC). "Citizenship" (politeuma) — we are citizens of the New Jerusalem, keeping correspondence with the world our home is in (MHC). "Lowly/vile body" — the body of our humiliation, to be transfigured (not changed in identity) to Christ's glorious form (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Reading the text as inspired and self-interpreting (WCF 1.9): v.21, cited by WCF 32.3 and WLC Q87, teaches that the just, by Christ's power and Spirit and by virtue of his resurrection as their head, shall be raised and made conformable to his glorious body. WLC Q127 (via v.17) grounds honoring superiors partly here in "imitation of their virtues and graces" — Paul's call to imitate; take only that imitation clause.

**Cross-references.** EPH.2.19 — fellow citizens with the saints; COL.3.1-3 — seek things above, life hid with Christ; 2CO.4.18-5.1 — unseen eternal things, heavenly building; 1JN.3.2 — we shall be like him; COL.3.4 — appear with him in glory; HEB.12.22 — come to the heavenly Jerusalem; 1CO.15.42-56 — the resurrection body; ROM.16.18 — serve their own bellies; 1PE.1.3-4 — inheritance kept in heaven; 2TI.4.8 — crown for those who love his appearing; ROM.8.29 — conformed to the Son's image; EPH.2.6 — seated with him; COL.2.6 — walk as you received him; ROM.8.5-7 — minding the flesh; 1TH.1.10 — await his Son from heaven.

**Reading moves.** Epistle: D7 = trace the contrast structure ("But our citizenship," v.20) that answers the "many" of vv.18-19; note the shift from exhortation-and-warning to eschatological hope. D3 on the argument's key terms — mature, enemies, citizenship, lowly/glorious body.
