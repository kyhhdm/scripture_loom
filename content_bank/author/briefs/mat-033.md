# Theological brief — MAT-033: The Authority of Jesus (MAT.7.28-29)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** These two verses are the narrator's conclusion to the whole Sermon on the Mount. The stress falls on the crowds' reaction — they "were astonished at His teaching" — and on the reason given for it: He taught "as one who had authority, and not as their scribes." The passage does not describe new content; it characterizes the *manner* and *source* of Jesus' teaching, setting Him over against the established teachers of Israel.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Astonished" — filled with wonder, though (MHC notes) one may admire good preaching yet remain unsanctified; astonishment is not yet faith. "Authority" — JFB: the consciousness of divine authority "as Lawgiver, Expounder and Judge" beamed through His teaching; His word was "a word of command," His lessons "law." "The scribes" — teachers with official standing whose preaching was, by contrast, "mean, and flat, and jejune," delivered as men not themselves masters of their message.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): receive the text as inspired and let Scripture interpret Scripture. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrinal statement is asserted here. The parallels confirm the plain sense — the astonishment at an authority the scribes lacked.

**Reading moves (per dimension).** Standard narrative frame, but note the comparative structure: D7 = trace the contrast "authority... not as the scribes," and ask what the crowd perceived. D3 on "authority" and "astonished" — attend to the manner, not merely the matter, of teaching.
