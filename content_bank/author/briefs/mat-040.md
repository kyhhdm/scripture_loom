# Theological brief — MAT-040: Jesus Heals a Paralytic (MAT 9:1–8)

**Passage's own emphasis.** The healing serves a greater claim: that "the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins." Jesus first pronounces the paralytic's sins forgiven; the scribes silently charge blasphemy; Jesus, knowing their thoughts, argues from what is visible to what is invisible — the easier-to-verify cure proves the harder-to-see authority. The bodily healing is deliberately made evidence of the pardoning power. The passage moves from faith brought to Jesus, through pardon and controversy, to public cure and the crowd glorifying God.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Their faith* — MHC reads it as strong, humble, and active, belonging to both the friends and the paralytic. *Son / be of good cheer* — a kind compellation and gracious encouragement to one afraid of rebuke. *Thy sins are forgiven* — the remission that is "the comfort of recovery," since sin is the cause of sickness. *Blasphemy* — the scribes' brand on heaven's grace. *Which is easier* — the cure of the body proving power over sin.

**Doctrinal anchors.** No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so none is claimed. By WCF ch.1 method the text is received as inspired and sufficient, interpreted by clearer Scripture. Note MHC's care: Jesus reads the scribes' hearts, which "no mere man could"; yet the crowd admired him "not as God... but as a man to whom God had given such power" — preserve that they did not yet confess his deity.

**Reading moves.** D7 = follow the embedded a fortiori argument; note the pivot at v6 ("But so that you may know...") where sign turns into proof. D3 on "authority to forgive sins" and "Son of Man." Otherwise standard narrative.
