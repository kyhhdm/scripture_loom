# Theological Brief — MAT-013: The Sermon on the Mount, setup (Matthew 5:1-2)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** A brief narrative setup, not teaching content itself: Jesus *saw the crowds*, *went up on the mountain*, *sat down*; his *disciples came to him*; he *opened his mouth* and began to teach. The emphasis is who is present (crowds, disciples), where (the mountain), and the orderly manner of beginning — seeing, ascending, sitting, being approached, then speaking.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Sat down" — signals a formal, solemn discourse ("he had placed himself so as to be best heard"), not a casual remark. "His disciples came to him" — Christ directs his speech to the disciples specifically, though the multitude is also within hearing. "Opened his mouth" — a solemn way of speaking, "preparing [the hearer] for something weighty."

**Doctrinal anchors.** No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; no doctrine is invented to fill this section. Per WCF 1 method only: this narrative is received as inspired and sufficient, its plain, historical sense standing without need of supplementation.
