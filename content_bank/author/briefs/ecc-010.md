# Theological Brief — ECC-010: The Vanity of Wealth Without Enjoyment (ECC.6.1-12)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** The Preacher names an evil "under the sun" that weighs heavily on mankind: God gives a man riches, wealth, and honor so he lacks nothing his heart desires, yet God does not grant him power to enjoy them — a stranger enjoys them instead (vv1-2). Such a man, however long-lived and full of children, is worse off than a stillborn child, for both go to one place, and only the miser knows the restless toil of an appetite never satisfied (vv3-7). Wealth without enjoyment profits nothing; better the sight of the eyes than the wandering of desire (vv8-9). What exists is named already; man cannot contend with One stronger, and his fleeting shadow-life keeps its future hidden (vv10-12).

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Futile / vanity* — vain because unused; what we do not use, we have in vain (MHC). *Power to eat* — the God-given capacity for enjoyment, here withheld as chastening for wealth's abuse (JFB). *Stranger* — those not akin, even hostile, to whom God gives up the covetous man's hoard (JFB). *Wandering of desire* — vague, insatiable craving for what one lacks, against the contented "sight of the eyes" (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Reading Scripture as inspired and self-interpreting (WCF 1.9-1.10), the passage exposes the sins of the covetous heart. WLC Q142 (cited via ECC.6.2) names "inordinate prizing and affecting worldly goods," "distrustful and distracting cares... in getting, keeping, and using them," and "defrauding ourselves of the due use and comfort of that estate which God hath given us" — precisely the miser's fault. WLC Q136 (cited via ECC.6.1-2) contributes only its reading of "distracting cares" and the sinful neglect of lawful means of preserving life; its wider sixth-commandment agenda is set aside.

**Cross-references.**
- ECC.6.2 -> ECC.5.19 — the God-given power to enjoy, here withheld.
- ECC.6.7 -> PRO.16.26 — labor is for the mouth, which craves it.
- ECC.6.7 -> 1TI.6.6-8 — godliness with contentment; food and raiment enough.
- ECC.6.9 -> ECC.1.14 — the wandering desire is vanity and vexation of spirit.
- ECC.6.10 -> ISA.45.9-10 — man may not contend with One mightier than he.
- ECC.6.12 -> JAS.4.14 — life a fleeting shadow, its future unknown.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** D7 = trace the observation-to-verdict pattern ("I have seen... this is futile"), and mark the shift at vv3-6 where the argument runs by comparison (the stillborn "better than") before turning to rhetorical questions from v8. D3 = fix the recurring terms *vanity/futile*, *under the sun*, *enjoy/power to eat*, tracking how "enjoyment" is God's gift, not wealth's yield.

Off-agenda set aside: WLC Q136's broader sixth-commandment scope (only its "distracting cares" reading is used).
