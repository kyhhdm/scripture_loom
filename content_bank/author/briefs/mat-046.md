# Theological brief — MAT-046: The Lord of the Harvest (MAT.9.35–38)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus finishes a whole circuit of teaching, preaching the gospel of the kingdom, and healing (v35). Seeing the crowds, He is "moved with compassion" because they are harassed and helpless, "like sheep without a shepherd" (v36). From that compassion He names the situation — "the harvest is plentiful, but the workers are few" (v37) — and gives one command: "Ask the Lord of the harvest… to send out workers into His harvest" (v38). The stress falls on Christ's compassion producing a summons to prayer for laborers.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Moved with compassion* — a spiritual, not merely temporal, pity for souls "ready to perish for lack of vision" (MHC). *Sheep without a shepherd* — Israel neglected under pharisaic guidance, their souls uncared for (JFB). *Harvest… plenteous* — souls ready to be gathered, the field widening toward the world (JFB). *Labourers* — men "divinely qualified and called," whom it is God's work to send (JFB, MHC). *Lord of the harvest* — "the great Lord and Proprietor of all" (JFB), who orders the work.

**Doctrinal anchors.** By WCF 1, receive this as inspired and sufficient. WLC Q191 (cited via v38), expounding "Thy kingdom come," reads this summons as prayer that "the gospel [be] propagated throughout the world" and "the church furnished with all gospel officers." Only that laborers-for-the-harvest sense is on-agenda here.

**Reading moves.** Standard narrative; D7 = trace the "therefore" of v38 back to the compassion of v36 (motive → command); D3 on "compassion," "harvest," "labourers." Watch the shift from Christ acting (teaching/healing, v35) to Christ commanding prayer (v38).
