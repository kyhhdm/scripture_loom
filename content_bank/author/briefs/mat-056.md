# Theological brief — MAT-056: Woe to the Unrepentant (Matthew 11:20–24)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus begins to denounce the very cities — Chorazin, Bethsaida, Capernaum — where most of His miracles were done, "because they did not repent." The one charge is impenitency, and its gravity rises with privilege: the places that saw the most mighty works are the most guilty. By comparison, pagan Tyre, Sidon, and even Sodom would have repented under such works; therefore it will be "more bearable" for them on the day of judgment. Greater light, refused, means severer reckoning.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Denounce / upbraid* — MHC: Christ "gives liberally, and upbraideth not," and reproaches only after gentler means are slighted. *Repented not* — sin against the gospel itself, the "remedial law"; willful impenitency, "the great damning sin of multitudes that enjoy the gospel." *Mighty works* — the miracles, an aggravation because they were the fullest discovery of grace. *Exalted to heaven … brought down to Hades* — Capernaum, Christ's usual residence, "the most favored spot"; privilege abused sinks the sinner lower. *More bearable … day of judgment* — degrees of misery fixed at the final, irretrievable judgment (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): receive this as the inspired, sufficient words of Christ; let Scripture read Scripture. Doctrine: WCF 3.2 (via 11.21) — the conditional "would have repented" reveals God's knowledge of what "may or can come to pass upon all supposed conditions," yet He "hath not decreed anything because he foresaw it as future"; MHC concurs, "God is a debtor to no man." WLC Q151 (via 11.21–24) — sin is aggravated by the means, mercies, and light enjoyed; impenitency under the fullest means is thus most heinous.

**Reading moves.** Genre: prophetic woe-oracle within narrative pronouncement. D7 = trace the comparative logic ("if the miracles … had been performed … they would have repented," so "more bearable … than for you"): the argument turns on refused privilege, not on the pagan cities' relative virtue. Note the shift at v23, where the paired woes give way to Capernaum's single height-to-depth reversal (Isaiah 14 echo). D3 = the weight of *repent*, *mighty works*, *upbraid*, and *bearable/tolerable*.

Set aside as off-agenda: WLC Q151's aggravations from persons, time, and place, and WCF 3.2's fuller treatment of the decree — cited here only for the reading of foreseen conditions and privilege-heightened guilt.
