# Theological brief — MAT-022: Giving to the Needy (MAT.6.1-4)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Christ opens a new section: beware of practicing "your righteous acts before men to be seen by them," or "you will have no reward from your Father in heaven." Almsgiving is the first instance. The hypocrites "sound a trumpet" in synagogues and streets "to be honored by men" — and "they already have their full reward." Against this, the disciple gives so secretly that "your left hand" does not know "what your right hand is doing," and "your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you." The stress falls on the motive: giving for God's approval, not men's applause.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Hypocrites" — those who "act a part," pretending to be what they are not, giving "purely for ostentation" (JFB, MHC). "Sound a trumpet" — figurative for "blazoning" the deed (JFB). "They have their reward" — a "receipt in full"; they wanted human applause and have it, with nothing "reserved for them in the future state" (MHC). "In secret" — conceal it, not observing it "too much ourselves," lest it "minister to spiritual pride" (JFB, MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired, self-interpreting Scripture (WCF 1.9). WLC Q113 (cited via 6.1-2) forbids "making profession of religion in hypocrisy, or for sinister ends" — the very fault here. WCF 16.7 (cited via 6.2) warns that works not done "to a right end, the glory of God" cannot please Him; yet almsgiving remains "a great duty" the disciple must abound in (MHC).

**Reading moves.** D7 = trace the contrast structure — "as the hypocrites do… but when you give" — reading each practice back to its motive and its reward (men's applause now vs. the Father's reward). D3 on "hypocrites," "reward," and "in secret," the terms the whole antithesis turns on.
