# Theological brief — MAT-051: Confessing Christ (MAT.10.32-33)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus states a two-sided rule with exact
symmetry: "everyone who confesses Me before men, I will also confess him before My
Father in heaven; but whoever denies Me before men, I will also deny him before My
Father." The stress falls on the reciprocity — a person's public owning or
disowning of Christ *before men* is answered, in kind, by Christ's owning or
disowning of that person *before the Father*. The saying closes a section on the
hostility Christ's servants will face, so "before men" carries the weight of
confessing under pressure.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Confess me before men* — JFB: to acknowledge
Christ openly, "despising the shame." *I will confess him before my Father* — Christ
"will not be ashamed of him, but will own him before the most august of all
assemblies." *Deny me* / *I will also deny* — before "that same assembly": each will
"have from Me his own treatment of Me on the earth."

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): received as the inspired words of Christ,
sufficient and self-interpreting; the parallel clauses interpret each other. Doctrine
(WLC Q90, cited via MAT.10.32): at the day of judgment the righteous are "openly
acknowledged and acquitted" and set at Christ's right hand — the future, public
"confessing before the Father" this verse promises. Use only that acknowledging
clause; Q90's fuller account of glory is off-agenda here.

**Reading moves.** Dominical saying in antithetical parallelism. D7 = trace the
mirrored clauses (confess→confessed, deny→denied) and the shift of arena from
"before men" to "before My Father." D3 on *confess*, *deny*, *before men*.

Off-agenda, set aside: WLC Q90's fuller description of resurrection glory and the
saints judging angels — cited here only for its "openly acknowledged" clause.
