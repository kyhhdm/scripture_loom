# MAT-025 — Treasures in Heaven (MAT.6.19-21)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus sets two treasuries against each other and commands a choice. Do *not* store treasure on earth, where moth, rust, and thieves make it perishable and precarious; *do* store treasure in heaven, where none of these can reach it. The point lands in v21: your heart follows your treasure. This is not merely counsel about money but a diagnosis of where the affections are lodged; the treasure chosen determines the bent of the whole inner life.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Treasure* — the "chief good," the one thing the heart most values and depends on for the future (MHC). *Moth and rust* — a clothes-moth and any "eating into" or wear-and-tear; together they show how perishable earthly wealth is (JFB). *Where your treasure is* — "that which ye value most" (JFB); the heart follows it "as the needle follows the loadstone" (MHC). Christ does not forbid treasure but directs its choice.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): receive these as the words of Christ, inspired and authoritative, and let clearer Scripture interpret them. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrinal anchor is asserted here.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** D7 = image → referent: read moth/rust/thieves as figures for the perishability of earthly goods, then trace to the heart-claim in v21 ("For where…"). D3 = weigh the two senses of *treasure* (possession vs. the good the heart rests in).

Off-agenda, set aside: vv22-24 (single eye, two masters) supplied context in the commentary but lie outside this pericope.
