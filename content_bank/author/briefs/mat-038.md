# Theological Brief — MAT-038: Jesus Calms the Storm (MAT 8:23-27)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** The disciples follow Jesus into the boat; a violent storm engulfs them while He sleeps. They wake Him crying "Lord, save us! We are perishing!" He answers their fear before the wind — "You of little faith, why are you so afraid?" — then rebukes the winds and sea into a perfect calm. The passage moves the disciples (and reader) to the closing question: "What kind of man is this? Even the winds and the sea obey Him!" The stress is on Jesus' identity, disclosed by His absolute command over creation, and on the contrast between His serene rest and their fearful faith.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Followed Him* — the true disciples are those willing to go to sea with Him, into danger, not only the land-way to heaven. *Asleep* — not Jonah's sleep of security but a "holy serenity," proving Him truly man, weary yet without the guilt or fear that disturbs repose. *Little faith* — real but weak faith; He chides them not for praying but for their fears, which dishonor Him. *Rebuked* — He stills the sea with a word alone (Moses used a rod), showing "absolute dominion over all the creatures." *What kind of man* — wonder at One who commands winds and seas.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired and sufficient (WCF 1.4-1.6), the narrative teaches by its own plain account: this is God the Son exercising the Creator's command over wind and sea. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; add no doctrine the lampposts do not supply. Scripture interprets Scripture — the disciples' amazed question is answered by the psalms of God's rule over the sea.

**Reading moves.** Standard narrative; D3/D7 well served by the plain account. If pressed, D7 = let the closing question ("what kind of man is this?") drive the reading, and note the shift from Jesus rebuking the disciples' fear to rebuking the sea.
