# Theological Brief — ECC-011: A Good Name and Sober Reflection

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Ecclesiastes 7:1–10 gathers "better than" sayings that overturn the world's reckoning: a good name outweighs fine perfume, the day of death the day of birth, mourning the house of feasting, sorrow the reckless laughter of fools, a wise man's rebuke the fool's song. The passage presses the living to "take this to heart" — seriousness before God does the heart more good than mirth. It then turns to endurance: the end of a matter is better than its beginning, a patient spirit better than a proud one; do not be quickly provoked, and do not idealize "the old days." Wisdom is grave, patient, and content under God's ordering.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Good name* — a name for wisdom and goodness that outlasts riches and ointment (MHC). *House of mourning* — the wise man's school, where mortality is laid to heart (MHC). *Sorrow better than laughter* — serious thoughts of eternity, by which "the heart is made better" (MHC/JFB). *Laughter of the fool* — crackling thorns: loud, flashy, soon gone (JFB). *Patient spirit vs. proud* — humility and waiting patience that let the wise bear oppression (MHC/JFB on v7–8).

**Doctrinal anchors.** No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrine is asserted here. Per WCF ch.1, read the text as inspired and sufficient, interpreting Scripture by Scripture: its plain wisdom-sayings are the rule, and the clearer New Testament links below open their full sense.
