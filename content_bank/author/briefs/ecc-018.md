# Theological Brief — ECC-018: Remember Your Creator (ECC 12:1-14)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** The Teacher presses one urgent charge: "Remember your Creator in the days of your youth" — *before* the failing days arrive. An extended figurative portrait of aging (vv.2-5) and death (vv.6-7) supplies the reason for the haste: the body returns to dust, the spirit returns to the God who gave it. Repeating his theme "everything is futile" (v.8), he commends his own words as accurate, delightful, and truthful (vv.9-11), then reaches the book's summit: "Fear God and keep His commandments... For God will bring every deed into judgment" (vv.13-14). Remembering the Creator now is the whole answer to a world that satisfies nothing.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Remember your Creator* — not merely recall that God made us, but render Him the honor owed the rightful Lord, and do it early (MHC: "the days of thy choice"). *The evil days / years of no pleasure* — the calamities of age and death (JFB, MHC), not a treatise but a warning to act while faculties are prime. *Silver cord... golden bowl* — figures of the dissolving body at death (JFB). *Goads... nails driven by a single Shepherd* — the wise words prick and fix the heart, ultimately given by the one divine Shepherd (MHC, JFB). *Fear God* — reverence and worship reigning in the heart, ruled by His commandments (MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Treated as inspired, sufficient, self-interpreting (WCF 1). *Via v.7:* WCF 4.2 / WLC Q17 — God formed man's body of dust and endued him with an immortal soul; WCF 32.1 — at death the body returns to dust while the soul immediately returns to God who gave it. *Via v.1:* WLC Q104 — the first commandment requires "remembering, highly esteeming... fearing" God, which this text urges in youth. *Via v.14:* WCF 33.1 — God has appointed a day to judge all persons by Jesus Christ, "according to what they have done in the body, whether good or evil."

**Cross-references.** DEU.10.12 — fear God and keep His commandments echoed; MIC.6.8 / DEU.6.2 — the same summary of true duty; PSA.111.10 / PRO.1.7 — fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom; 2CO.5.10 — every deed judged before Christ's tribunal; LAM.3.27 — good to bear the yoke in youth; PSA.71.17-18 — God remembered from youth; PRO.22.6 — training in the young; ECC.8.12 — it goes well with those who fear God; JOB.28.28 — the fear of the Lord is wisdom.

**Reading moves (D3/D7).** Wisdom-poetry. D7 = image → referent: read vv.2-6 as figures for aging and death (darkened lights, trembling keepers, snapped silver cord), not literal scenes; note the shift at v.6 from *old age* to *death itself*, then the plain prose conclusion at vv.13-14. D3 = weight the recurring terms *remember*, *futile/vanity*, and *fear God* as the argument's hinges.

*Off-agenda, set aside: WLC Q136 (sixth-commandment sins), cited via v.12, contributes only its reading of immoderate/wearying labor; its wider content on taking life is not this passage's concern.*
