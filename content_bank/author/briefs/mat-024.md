# Theological Brief — MAT-024: Proper Fasting (Matthew 6:16-18)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus treats fasting as a real, assumed duty of His disciples ("when you fast," not "if"), then governs its *manner*. The contrast is between the hypocrites, who disfigure their faces so men will see them fasting and thereby "already have their full reward," and the disciple, who anoints his head and washes his face so the fast is hidden from men and seen only by the Father who is unseen. The stress falls on the audience of the act: fasting is directed to the Father who sees in secret, and He rewards.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Fast* — an act of self-denial and humbling of the soul (Ps 35:13), a means to dispose us for other duties, not an end in itself. *Disfigure their faces* — literally "make unseen," a slovenly, affected, downcast appearance to attract notice. *Hypocrites* — those who pretend the outward show while lacking the inward contrition that is the life of the duty; theirs are "mock-fasts." *Anoint... wash* — appear as usual, as on ordinary days, so the devotion is concealed. *In secret* — the fast owned by God's omniscience alone.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired and sufficient (WCF 1), the text lets Scripture interpret Scripture: the reward-seeking display is exposed by other places. WCF 16.7 (cited via v.16) bears on the passage only at its point of contact: works not done "to a right end, the glory of God" cannot please Him — the hypocrite's fast, aimed at men's praise, is such a work; yet WCF 16.7 also warns that *neglect* of commanded duties is more sinful, so the passage rebukes the manner, not the duty. WLC Q113 (cited via v.16) names among third-commandment sins "making profession of religion in hypocrisy, or for sinister ends" — precisely the disfigured face fasting for reputation.

**Reading moves (per dimension).** D7 = trace the two "when you fast" clauses and the resulting contrast of audiences (men vs. the Father who sees in secret); note the reward reversal — "they already have their full reward" (v.16) against "your Father... will reward you" (v.18). D3 on *disfigure* (commentary: "make unseen"), *anoint/wash* (appear as usual), and *in secret*.
