# Theological Brief — MAT-042: Questions about Fasting (MAT 9:14–15)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** John's disciples ask why they and the Pharisees fast often while Jesus' disciples do not. Jesus answers not by condemning fasting but by naming the *season*: the guests of the bridegroom cannot mourn while the bridegroom is with them. His bodily presence is itself the joy that makes present fasting improper. Yet He foretells that "the bridegroom will be taken from them; then they will fast." The stress falls on Christ Himself as the bridegroom whose presence and coming absence set the proper time for joy and for mourning.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Bridegroom* — Christ, of whom His disciples are "the children of the bride-chamber," welcome at the wedding-feast. *Mourn / fast* — religious fasting, an act of devotion whose fitness depends on the season, not a merit to boast in. *Taken from them* — His coming removal (imprisonment foreshadowed in John, then the passion), when mourning and fasting become seasonable.

**Doctrinal anchors.** By WCF 1.9, Scripture interprets Scripture: Jesus draws on the bridegroom image John had already used (John 3:29), so the disciples could answer themselves. WCF 21.5 lists "solemn fastings" among worship "in their several times and seasons, to be used in an holy and religious manner" — teaching precisely the seasonableness the passage stresses, not that fasting is meritorious.

**Reading moves (per dimension).** D7 = image → referent: read *bridegroom* as Christ and *children of the bride-chamber* as His disciples, then trace the shift within v15 from present joy ("while He is with them") to future mourning ("taken from them; then they will fast"). D3 on *fast* and *mourn* — devotional acts whose meaning here is governed by season, not quantity.
