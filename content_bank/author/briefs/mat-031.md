# Theological brief — MAT-031: A Tree and Its Fruit (MAT 7:15-23)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus warns His disciples to beware false prophets who come disguised as sheep but are inwardly ravening wolves. The test He gives is fruit: a good tree cannot bear bad fruit, a bad tree cannot bear good — so "by their fruit you will recognize them." The passage then presses the same standard inward: not everyone who says "Lord, Lord" enters the kingdom, but only he who does the Father's will. Even prophesying, casting out demons, and miracles in Christ's name save no one; to such He says, "I never knew you; depart from Me, you workers of lawlessness." Profession without obedience is exposed.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *False prophets* — false teachers, whether producing false commissions or false doctrine (MHC). *Sheep's clothing* — a fair, plausible, harmless exterior masking malicious design (MHC/JFB). *Fruit* — chiefly the practical effect of their teaching and the tenor of a life, not particular acts (JFB, MHC). *Lord, Lord* — a genuine title Christ expects, yet mere formality without doing the Father's will is empty (JFB). *I never knew you* — never owned as His; "there never was any" acquaintance (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive Christ's words as the inspired, sufficient rule of faith and life; let Scripture interpret Scripture. Doctrine: WLC Q61 (via v21) — not all who hear the gospel and live in the visible church are saved, but only true members of the church invisible. WCF 10.4 and WLC Q68 (via v22) — the non-elect may be outwardly called and share common operations of the Spirit, yet never truly come to Christ. WCF 18.1 (via v22-23) — hypocrites may deceive themselves with false hopes that shall perish. WLC Q180 (via v21) — praying in Christ's name is not the bare mentioning of it.

**Cross-references.** ROM 2:13 — doers, not hearers, are justified. LUK 6:46 — "why call Me Lord and not do?" JAS 1:22 — be doers, not hearers only. LUK 11:28 — blessed are those who hear and keep the word. TIT 1:16 — profess to know God but deny Him by works. MAT 25:11-12 — "Lord, Lord... I do not know you." 1JN 4:1 — test the spirits. 2PE 2:1-3 / MAT 24:11 — false teachers/prophets arise. MAT 10:32-33 — confessing and denying before the Father.

**Reading moves.** D7 = image -> referent: read the tree/fruit metaphor (v16-20) as the visible life disclosing the inward heart, then note the shift at v21 from testing others to self-examination. D3 on the passage's freighted terms — "false prophet," "fruit," "Lord, Lord," "I never knew you" — whose weight the commentary supplies.

Off-agenda: WLC Q180's fuller treatment of prayer's boldness and hope of acceptance, and WCF 18.1's positive assurance for true believers, are set aside; only their bearing on hollow profession is used here.
