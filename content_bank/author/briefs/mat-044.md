# Theological Brief — MAT-044: The Healing Touch of Jesus (Matthew 9:18-26)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Two miracles are interwoven: a synagogue ruler kneels, saying his daughter has just died, yet asks Jesus to come lay His hand on her so she "will live"; on the way, a woman bleeding twelve years touches the fringe of His cloak and is healed. The passage stresses Christ's power over both incurable disease and death itself, and the faith that reaches for Him. Jesus dismisses the mourners — "not dead, but asleep" — is laughed at, then takes the girl by the hand and she rises. Christ, unlike the Levitical priest, touches the dead and is not defiled; He is Lord over death.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Daughter" — a tender, fatherly word of comfort, the same tenderness shown the paralytic called "son." "Thy faith hath made thee whole" — the honor Christ places on faith; the cure is the fruit and effect of faith, not the touch's mechanics. "Not dead but sleepeth" — death as sleep: short in continuance, a rest in hope for those who die in the Lord. "Took her by the hand" — raised not by prayer, as Elijah and Elisha, but by a touch, as Lord over souls.

**Doctrinal anchors.** No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; per WCF 1.9, let Scripture interpret Scripture. The parallel accounts open the passage's own sense; no doctrine is added beyond what the text bears.
