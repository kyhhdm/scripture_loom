# Theological Brief — PHP-010: Paul's Contentment and Closing Greetings (PHP.4.10-23)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Paul closes by thanking the Philippians for their gift, but reframes it: he rejoices in the Lord that their care has "revived," yet insists he does not speak "in respect of want" (v11). The center is a learned contentment in every circumstance—"abased" or "abounding," filled or hungry—grounded not in himself but in Christ who strengthens him (v13). He values their gift chiefly as "fruit that may abound to your account" (v17), an "acceptable sacrifice, well-pleasing to God" (v18), and pledges that God will supply their need. The passage ends in doxology (v20), greetings, and benediction.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Content* (v11)—JFB: sufficiency not in self but in God, raised above the Stoic's self-sufficiency. *Abased / abound* (v12)—MHC: an equal temper accommodating every condition, each with its own temptations. *Strengtheneth me* (v13)—MHC notes the present, continued act: Christ's constant, renewed strength, not Paul's own. *Fragrant offering* (v18)—a sacrifice of acknowledgment, not of atonement.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive the text as inspired and sufficient, letting Scripture interpret Scripture. Doctrine: WCF 16.3 (cited via PHP.4.13)—believers' ability to do good works is "not at all of themselves, but wholly from the Spirit of Christ," requiring an actual influence of the Spirit; yet keep the balancing clause—they are not therefore to "grow negligent," but "diligent in stirring up the grace of God that is in them."

**Reading moves.** Epistle: D7 = trace the connectives—the "not that... but" of vv11, 17 reframes the gift from Paul's need to their spiritual fruit; note the pivot at v13 where boasting turns to dependence on Christ. D3 = weigh the argument's key terms (*content*, *abased*, *abound*, *fruit*, *sacrifice*).

Set aside as off-agenda: none—WCF 16.3's clauses on the necessity of the Spirit and on continued diligence both fit v13's plain sense.
