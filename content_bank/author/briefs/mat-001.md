# Theological brief — MAT-001: The Genealogy of Jesus (MAT.1.1-17)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Matthew opens his Gospel as a "book of the generation" — a documentary pedigree tracing Jesus Christ from Abraham through David down to Joseph, "of whom was born Jesus, who is called Christ" (v16). Its point is titular and legal: to prove that Jesus is "the son of David, the son of Abraham" (v1), and therefore of the nation and family from which Messiah must arise. The whole is deliberately ordered into three fourteens — Abraham to David, David to the exile, the exile to Christ (v17) — an aid to memory marking rising, flourishing, and declining eras that meet in Jesus.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Genealogy / book of the generation* — a pedigree "given in evidence, to prove a title" (MHC). *Son of David, son of Abraham* — the two great trustees of the Messianic promise, of dominion and of blessing. *David the king* — alone titled "king" (v6), the line whose throne Messiah inherits. *Christ / Anointed* — the Messiah consecrated, as king, priest, and prophet were, to his office (JFB). *The exile to Babylon* — Matthew's "migration," softening the bitter word (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive this text as God-inspired and sufficient, letting Scripture interpret Scripture — the promises to Abraham and David (GEN.22.18; PSA.132.11) find here their fulfillment, God faithful though performance was long deferred. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrinal claim is drawn beyond the passage.

**Reading moves.** Straightforward narrative/list; D3/D7 standard. One aid: D7 = read the two named titles (v1) as the passage's thesis, and the four women and the omitted kings as commentary-noted marks of grace, not gaps to correct.

Off-agenda, set aside: speculative harmonizing of the omitted kings and of Salathiel/Zerubbabel's line; the allegory of Phares and Zara — grounding detail only, not doctrine to assert.
