# Theological brief — MAT-029: Ask, Seek, Knock (MAT.7.7-12)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus commands persistent prayer in three
mounting terms — ask, seek, knock — and answers each with a matching promise of
success (given, find, opened), then repeats and widens it: "everyone who asks
receives." He grounds this in the Father's goodness by an argument from the
lesser to the greater: earthly fathers, though evil, give bread not stones, fish
not snakes; "how much more" will the Father in heaven give good things to those
who ask Him. Verse 12 draws the "therefore" — do to others as you would have
them do to you — as the essence of the Law and the Prophets.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Ask, seek, knock* — a rising importunity: we
ask for what we wish, seek for what we miss, knock at what we feel shut out from
(JFB); seeking and knocking add endeavor and perseverance to asking (MHC).
*Good things* — what the wise Father judges fit, denying what would harm
(MHC: "denials in love are better than grants in anger"). *Law and the
Prophets* — the golden rule as "all Scripture in a nutshell" (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive this as the inspired Word, the
Father's own promise, sufficient and self-consistent. Doctrine: WLC Q184 (cited
via 7.11) — we pray for things "tending to the glory of God, the welfare of the
church, our own or others' good; but not for anything that is unlawful,"
matching Christ's "good things." WLC Q122 (cited via 7.12) — the sum of duty to
man is "to do to others what we would have them to do to us."

**Cross-references.** MAT.21.22 — believing prayer receives; 1JN.5.14-15 —
asking according to His will; JHN.15.7 — abiding prayer answered; MRK.11.24 —
believe you have received; JER.29.12-13 — seek and find with the whole heart;
JER.33.3 — call and He answers; PSA.145.18-19 — near to all who call; HEB.11.6 —
He rewards those who seek Him; MAT.6.33 — seek first the kingdom; LUK.11.9-13 —
the parallel, the Father giving; LUK.6.31 — the golden rule restated.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** D7 = trace the "how much
more" argument (v9-11) from human fathers to the heavenly Father, and the
"therefore/then" linking prayer (v7-11) to the golden rule (v12). D3 on the
rising trio ask/seek/knock and on "good things." Note the shift at v12 from
prayer Godward to justice manward.

Off-agenda, set aside: WLC Q184's fuller catalog of prayer's objects beyond
Christ's "good things" here; the following strait-gate section (v13-14) in the
MHC lamppost, outside this pericope.
