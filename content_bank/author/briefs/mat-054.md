# Theological brief — MAT-054: John's Inquiry (MAT 11:1-6)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Having finished instructing the Twelve, Jesus goes on to teach and preach; meanwhile John, hearing in prison of "the works of Christ," sends disciples to ask, "Are You the One who was to come, or should we look for someone else?" Jesus gives no bare declaration but points to what they hear and see: the blind see, the lame walk, lepers are cleansed, the deaf hear, the dead are raised, the poor hear good news. The passage stresses that Christ's identity is established by deeds that fulfill prophecy, and closes with a blessing on the one who does not fall away over Him.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "He that should come" — a recognized Old Testament name for the promised Messiah (Psa 118:26); the question is whether Jesus is He. "The works of Christ" — miracles as the "broad seal of heaven," both a divine power and the accomplishment of prediction (Isa 35). "Offended / fall away" — scandalized; Matthew Henry notes the mean appearance of Christ tempts even good men (perhaps John himself) to stumble, so faith may hold a "mixture of unbelief"; blessed are they who overcome the offense.

**Doctrinal anchors.** By WCF ch.1, Scripture is God-breathed, sufficient, and self-interpreting: Jesus answers John by echoing Isaiah, letting prophecy identify its fulfillment. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrinal anchor is asserted beyond this method.

**Reading moves.** D7 = let the works answer the question — trace each clause of v5 back to its Isaiah referent, so the passage interprets itself. D3 on "he that should come," "works," and "offended," which carry the argument. Note the shift from question (v3) to evidentiary reply (v4-5) to beatitude (v6).

Set aside as off-agenda: the disciplinary details of John's imprisonment (Herod) beyond their bearing on his question.
