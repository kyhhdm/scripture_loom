# Theological Brief — MAT-045: Jesus Heals the Blind and Mute (MAT.9.27-34)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Two paired miracles display Christ's power and the responses it provokes. Two blind men follow Him crying "Son of David, have mercy on us," and He first tests their faith — "Do you believe that I am able to do this?" — then heals with "According to your faith will it be done to you," charging them to keep silent. Then a mute demoniac is brought; when the demon is driven out, the man speaks. The crowds marvel ("Nothing like this has ever been seen in Israel"), but the Pharisees blaspheme ("by the prince of demons"). The passage stresses faith as the condition of Christ's mercy, and the dividing effect of His works.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Son of David* — the well-known Messianic title; their faith fastens on the promise that Messiah would open blind eyes. *Have mercy on us* — needing only a share in the Lord's tender mercy. *According to your faith* — not a cure proportioned to faith, but granted to it; Christ knows and approves their sincere faith. *Demon-possessed and mute* — the dumbness is the effect of the possession, not natural.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1.9): Scripture interprets Scripture; these inspired, sufficient narratives are read by clearer places. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrinal statement is asserted from it.

**Reading moves.** Standard narrative; D7 = trace the two responses (crowds marvel vs. Pharisees blaspheme) to the one point that faith receives Christ's mercy. D3 on *Son of David* and *according to your faith*.
