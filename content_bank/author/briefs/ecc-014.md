# Theological Brief — ECC-014: Wisdom, Death, and Chance (ECC 9:1-18)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Solomon fixes first on a settled truth — "the righteous and the wise, as well as their deeds, are in God's hands" (v1) — then concedes the hard fact that unsettles observers: one common fate, death, comes alike to righteous and wicked, clean and unclean (vv2-3). Because the dead have no further portion "under the sun" (vv5-6), the living, who still have hope (v4), are urged to receive food, drink, marriage, and labor as God's approved gift with a cheerful heart (vv7-9), and to do whatever their hand finds "with all their might," since there is no work in Sheol (v10). Outcomes, though, are not commanded by strength or skill: "time and chance happen to all" (v11), and no one knows his time (v12). The closing vignette (vv13-18) commends wisdom — better than strength and weapons — even when a poor wise man's deliverance is despised and forgotten, and warns that "one sinner destroys much good."

**Key terms (from commentary).** *In God's hands* — under his special protection and guidance, events serving the saints' good (MHC). *One event / common fate* — death common to all, not the eternal state (JFB). *Live dog better than a dead lion* — the vilest living still have hope of repentance and salvation, the dead none (JFB). *Portion* — what the wicked had only in this life, now lost (JFB, Ps 17:14). *Time and chance* — seemingly casual, really Providence; "duties are ours, events God's" (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): receive the text as inspired and sufficient, and let Scripture interpret Scripture — "the dead know nothing" (v5) concerns bodily senses and worldly affairs, read by clearer places (JFB), not a doctrine of the soul's extinction. Doctrine: WLC Q113 (cited via v3) names among third-commandment sins "all sinful cursings, oaths, vows... violating of our oaths"; the passage's contrast between the one who swears rashly and the one who fears an oath supplies only this — reverence versus profaning God's name. No further doctrine is added.
