# Stage-1 Theological Brief — ECC-017: Diligence in an Uncertain Life (ECC.11.1-10)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** This passage presses generous, persistent action in a world we cannot predict. "Cast your bread upon the waters" and "divide your portion to seven, or even eight" (vv.1-2): give liberally and widely, for you do not know what disaster may come. The recurring refrain is human ignorance — you do not know the path of the wind, how bones form in the womb, or "the work of God, the Maker of all things" (v.5). Because outcomes are hidden, the one who watches wind and clouds never sows or reaps (v.4); instead, "sow your seed in the morning, and do not rest your hands in the evening," since you do not know which will prosper (v.6). Life's light is sweet, yet the "days of darkness" will be many (vv.7-8). Finally, the young are told to rejoice — then soberly warned, "for all these things God will bring you to judgment" (v.9).

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Cast thy bread upon the waters* — liberal charity sown like seed, trusted to God for a certain but delayed return (MHC). *Seven... eight* — the perfect number and beyond; give to many in need (JFB). *Evil / disaster* — a coming day when you may need those bound to you by kindness (JFB, Luk 16:9). *Work of God* — his unsearchable providence, no more traceable than the soul's entry into the body (MHC). *Sow morning and evening* — early and late, when young and old (JFB). *Rejoice, O young man* — warning, not permission; irony checked by judgment (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so none is asserted. Per WCF 1.9-1.10, this inspired text is interpreted by clearer Scripture: its plain wisdom-sense — diligent, generous faithfulness under a sovereign, inscrutable God who will judge — stands on its own authority as the Word of God.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** D7 = image -> life-principle; trace the agrarian and mercantile metaphors (bread on waters, sowing, wind/clouds) to their referent — liberal, persevering faithfulness amid ignorance — then note the shift at v.7 from doing good to facing death and judgment. D3 on *cast thy bread*, *portion*, *days of darkness*, and the ironic "rejoice."
