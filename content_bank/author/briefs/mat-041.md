# Theological Brief — MAT-041: Jesus Calls Matthew (MAT 9:9-13)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Christ's grace reaches out first: He sees a tax collector "sitting at the tax booth," says "Follow Me," and Matthew rises and follows at once. Jesus then dines with "many tax collectors and sinners." When Pharisees object to His company, He answers that the sick, not the healthy, need a physician, commands them to "go and learn" what "I desire mercy, not sacrifice" means, and states His errand: "I have not come to call the righteous, but sinners." The passage stresses Christ's sovereign, seeking call and His mission to sinners.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Matthew* — "the gift of God," the penman who humbly names himself by his publican name. *Sitting at the tax booth* — Christ calls the employed, as He did the fishermen; a calling of "ill fame" from which grace saves. *Physician / sick* — "Sin is the sickness of the soul," and Christ is "the great Physician of souls." *Mercy, not sacrifice* — mercy (compassion that lifts the fallen) preferred to mere ritual, "the one rather than the other," not sacrifice abolished.

**Doctrinal anchors.** No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; per WCF 1.9 the text is read by clearer Scripture, not by imported doctrine. The passage's own witness: Christ's call is effectual and preventing ("He is found of those that seek him not"), and His mission is to sinners who feel their need.

**Reading moves.** Straightforward narrative with an embedded controversy-saying; D3/D7 standard, except v12-13 where D7 = unfold the proverb ("physician") and the Hosea citation to their referent — mercy that seeks the fallen, not ritual purity.
