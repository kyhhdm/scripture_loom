# Theological brief — MAT-021: Love Your Enemies (Matthew 5:38–48)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus takes two received sayings — "eye for eye" and "love your neighbor / hate your enemy" — and, with his repeated "But I tell you," presses the law past its abused, minimal reading to its full inward reach. Against private revenge he sets non-retaliation: turn the cheek, yield the cloak, go the second mile, give and lend freely (vv. 38–42). Against selective love he sets love, blessing, and prayer for enemies (vv. 43–44). The ground is the Father, who sends sun and rain on evil and good alike; his children are to resemble him, doing more than tax collectors and Gentiles who love only their own, and so to be "perfect, as your heavenly Father is perfect" (vv. 45–48).

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Resist not evil* — not repealing self-preservation or the magistrate's sword, but forbidding rendering evil for evil and personal vengeance (MHC, JFB). *Love your enemies* — the word denotes moral love, the "benignant, compassionate outgoings of desire for another's good," not complacency in their character (JFB). *Perfect* — completeness of kind, the God-imitating fullness of doing good to the evil and unthankful, not sinless degree (JFB, MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired and sufficient, Scripture interpreting Scripture (WCF 1). WCF 19.4: the eye-for-eye judicial law expired with Israel's State, binding now only in its general equity — vengeance committed to the magistrate, not the private person. WLC Q99: the law is spiritual, reaching the affections and requiring the utmost perfection of every duty — hence enemy-love, not mere outward restraint. WLC Q7: the Father is "most merciful and gracious, long-suffering, and abundant in goodness," the model children imitate. WLC Q183: we are to pray "yea, [for] our enemies."

**Cross-references.** ROM.12.14 / 1PE.3.9 — bless, render not evil for evil. LUK.6.27-28, ROM.12.20-21, PRO.25.21-22 — do good, feed the enemy, heap coals. LUK.23.34 / ACT.7.60 / 1PE.2.23 — Christ and Stephen praying for persecutors, the pattern lived. LUK.6.34-35 — lend hoping for nothing, and be sons of the Most High. PSA.145.9 / ACT.14.17 — the Father's common goodness to all. 1PE.1.15-16, LEV.19.2, JAS.1.4 — be holy/complete as he is.

**Reading moves (per dimension).** D7: trace the twofold "You have heard... But I tell you" antithesis, and the "that you may be sons" logic grounding the command in the Father's character (vv. 45, 48). D3: hold the commentary's senses of *resist not*, *love*, and *perfect* against their flattened popular readings.

Set aside as off-agenda: WLC Q113 (third-commandment sins, cited only via the broader 5:21–48 span) contributes no doctrine to this passage's emphasis.
