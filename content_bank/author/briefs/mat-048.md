# Theological brief — MAT-048: The Ministry of the Twelve (MAT.10.5-15)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus commissions the Twelve and hands them their marching orders. He fixes their field ("Go rather to the lost sheep of Israel"; not yet Gentiles or Samaritans), their message ("The kingdom of heaven is near"), and their confirming works ("Heal the sick, raise the dead, cleanse the lepers, drive out demons"). "Freely you have received; freely give" governs how they minister — no gold, bag, or spare tunic, for "the worker is worthy of his provisions." Entering a town they seek the worthy, bless a house with peace, and where rejected shake off the dust, a town's refusal weighing heavier at judgment than Sodom's.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Lost sheep of Israel* — the covenant people abandoned by faithless shepherds, whom the first offer of salvation must reach (MHC, JFB). *Worthy* — not rank or wealth but "congenial disposition," those disposed to receive the gospel (JFB on v11). *Freely give* — free grace; power to heal not to be turned to gain (MHC). *Shake off the dust* — a symbolic severing, a testimony against those who reject the message (JFB, MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive this as the inspired, sufficient Word (1.4, 1.6); the mission's first-stage restriction to Israel is later lifted by Scripture itself (Acts 1:8), Scripture interpreting Scripture (1.9). MHC notes these confirming miracles attested a doctrine now settled, not a standing warrant for signs. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; no further doctrinal anchor is asserted.

**Cross-references.**
- MAT.10.8 -> ACT.3.6 — "freely give" enacted: silver and gold I do not have.
- MAT.10.8 -> LUK.10.9 — parallel commission: heal and announce the kingdom.
- MAT.10.8 -> ACT.20.33-35 — Paul's echo: more blessed to give than receive.
- MAT.10.6 -> JER.50.6 — Israel as lost sheep failed by their shepherds.
- MAT.10.7 -> MAT.3.2 — same kingdom-near proclamation as John and Jesus.
- MAT.10.14 -> ACT.13.51 — apostles shaking dust off against rejecters.
- MAT.10.14 -> ACT.18.6 — Paul enacting the same symbolic severance.
- MAT.10.5 -> JHN.4.9 — the Jew-Samaritan estrangement behind the restriction.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** Straightforward narrative-commission; D3/D7 largely standard. Light D3 on *worthy* (disposition, not status) and *shake off the dust* (symbolic testimony, not mere gesture). D7 note: the restriction to Israel is stage-specific to this first mission, read it in light of the later universal sending (Acts 1:8) rather than as a permanent limit.

Off-agenda, set aside: the wider chapter's teaching on future persecution (10:16-23) beyond this pericope's bounds.
