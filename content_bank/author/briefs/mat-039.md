# Theological Brief — MAT-039: The Demons and the Pigs (MAT.8.28-34)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** This narrative displays Christ's divine power over devils — the most formidable of all foes. Two violent, tomb-dwelling men, whom no one could subdue, are met by Jesus, whose mere presence and single word "Go!" drives the demons out. The demons confess Him as "Son of God," dread torment "before the appointed time," beg permission even to enter the pigs, and can do nothing without His leave. The herd's destruction shows what their possession always aims at. The town's response is the sting: they beg the Savior to leave.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *"Son of God"* — a true confession the demons make, yet "it is not knowledge, but love, that distinguishes saints from devils." *"Before the appointed time"* — the demons know a fixed day of fuller torment is coming and dread its nearness. *"Begged Him to leave"* — the townsfolk, valuing swine over the Savior, echo the demons' own "What have we to do with thee?"

**Doctrinal anchors.** Treating the text as inspired and sufficient (WCF 1): WLC Q19 (cited via MAT.8.31) teaches God's providence toward angels — He "permitted some of the angels, wilfully and irrecoverably, to fall," yet limits and orders their sin "to his own glory." The demons' need for permission fits exactly this: their power is real but wholly under Christ's control.
