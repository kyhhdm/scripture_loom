# Theological Brief — MAT-052: Not Peace but a Sword (Matthew 10:34–39)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus corrects a mistaken expectation: He did not come to bring earthly peace but "a sword" — division that cuts even through the household, setting father against son and daughter against mother. The passage then presses the cost of discipleship: whoever loves father, mother, son, or daughter more than Christ, or will not take up his cross and follow, "is not worthy of Me." It closes with the paradox that finding one's life means losing it, and losing life for Christ's sake means finding it.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Sword" — the strife and "deadly opposition between eternally hostile principles" the gospel provokes; the fault lies not in the gospel but in those who reject it. "Take up his cross" — in its primary sense a readiness "to go forth even to crucifixion," not merely trials in general. "Findeth/loseth his life" — the double sense of *life*, natural and temporal against spiritual and eternal; surrendering the lower is indispensable to preserving the higher.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): receive these as Christ's own inspired and sufficient words, and let Scripture interpret Scripture — the household strife of v.35–36 is quoted from Micah 7:6. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no specific doctrine is asserted from the standards here.

**Reading moves.** D7 = press the paradox: hold both senses of "life" (v.39) and both senses of "sword"/"cross" (literal readiness to die, not general hardship) without flattening either; note the shift from external division (v.34–36) to internal allegiance and self-denial (v.37–39). D3 = weigh the loaded terms *sword*, *cross*, *worthy*, *find/lose life* in the commentary's sense, not softened.

Off-agenda, set aside: the wider missionary-persecution frame of Matthew 10:16–33 (councils, kings, fearing God not man) that surrounds but is not this pericope.
