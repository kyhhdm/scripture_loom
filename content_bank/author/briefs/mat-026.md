# Theological brief — MAT-026: The Lamp of the Body (MAT 6:22-24)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Christ presses a single choice through three linked images. The eye is the body's lamp: a good (single) eye fills the whole body with light; a bad eye fills it with darkness, and "if the light within you is darkness, how great is that darkness!" (v.23). This drives to the flat maxim of v.24 — no one can serve two masters; he will love one and hate, or hold to one and despise, the other — applied bluntly: "You cannot serve both God and money." The stress is on undivided allegiance; a heart split between God and gain is not half-lit but wholly dark.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Single eye* — JFB: "simple, clear," singleness of purpose "looking right at its object, as opposed to having two ends in view." *Evil eye* — "distempered," a mind divided between heaven and earth, "all dark." *Serve* — "to belong wholly and be entirely under command to." *Mammon* — JFB, "what one trusts in," here riches "considered as an idol master, or god of the heart"; MHC widens it to whatever we account gain, ultimately "secular self."

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive this as God's inspired, sufficient Word and let Scripture interpret Scripture — Luke 11:34-36 and 16:13 open the parallel sense. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; assert no doctrine the lampposts do not supply.

**Cross-references.**
- 1JN 2:15-16 — do not love the world; its lusts contrast God's service.
- LUK 16:13 — the same maxim: God or money, not both.
- LUK 11:34-36 — parallel lamp/eye saying, the interpretive key.
- JAS 4:4 — friendship with the world is enmity with God.
- 1TI 6:9-10 — the love of money as a snare.
- JOS 24:15 — "choose this day whom you will serve."
- GAL 1:10 — cannot please men and be Christ's servant.
- MAT 4:10 — worship and serve God alone.
- ROM 6:16-22 — slaves of the one you obey.
- 2CO 11:3 — the mind led astray from single devotion.
- 1KI 18:21 — do not waver between two masters.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** D7 = image -> referent: read the eye/lamp as the heart's aim (single vs. divided), then trace the shift at v.24 from metaphor to the plain either/or maxim and its application to money. D3 on the loaded terms *single*, *evil eye*, *serve*, *mammon* — carrying the commentary's senses, not sharpening them.

Set aside as off-agenda: MHC's surrounding treatment of laying up treasure and hypocrisy in almsgiving (vv.19-21), which frames but is not this pericope's own stress.
