# MAT-016 — The Fulfillment of the Law (Matthew 5:17-20)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus states His relation to the Law and the Prophets: He has not come to abolish but to fulfill them. Their permanence is absolute — until heaven and earth pass, not a jot or stroke will disappear until everything is accomplished. Therefore how one treats even the least commandment measures greatness or smallness in the kingdom, and entry into the kingdom demands a righteousness surpassing that of the scribes and Pharisees.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Fulfill* (plērōsai): not to pour out but to fill the vessel to the brim — to obey, to confirm the promises and types, to complete and establish, not abrogate (MHC; JFB). *Abolish / destroy*: subvert, annul, make invalid. *Jot and tittle*: the smallest Hebrew letter and the little stroke distinguishing letters — even the least of God's word stands. *Break*: dissolve or enervate a command's obligation, and teach others so. *Righteousness that exceeds*: a superiority in kind, not degree — inward, vital, spiritual, over against the merely outward and formal (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Treating the text as inspired and self-interpreting (WCF 1): Scripture is "kept pure in all ages" and authentical (WCF 1.8, via v.18) — grounding the endurance of jot and tittle. Christ "was made under the law, and did perfectly fulfill it" (WCF 8.4; WLC Q48, via v.17). The moral law "doth forever bind all"; Christ "in the gospel [doth not] any way dissolve, but much strengthen, this obligation" (WCF 19.5, via vv.17-19).

**Cross-references.** ROM.10.4 — Christ the law's end/goal; ROM.3.31 — faith upholds, not overthrows, law; GAL.4.4-5 — born under law to redeem; ROM.8.4 — law's requirement met in us; LUK.16.17 — easier heaven and earth pass than one tittle fail; ISA.40.8 / 1PE.1.25 — the word endures forever; DEU.12.32 — add nothing, take nothing; JAS.2.10-11 — the law's unity; PHP.3.9 / ROM.9.30-32 — righteousness by faith, not Pharisaic works; ISA.42.21 — magnifying the law.

**Reading moves (per dimension).** D7 = trace the four-part logic: not-abolish → fulfill → therefore permanent → therefore obey-and-exceed; watch the pivot at v.20 from the law's standing to the hearer's required righteousness. D3 on the passage's load-bearing terms — *fulfill*, *jot/tittle*, *least*, *exceed* — where the whole claim turns.

*Off-agenda (set aside): the Sabbath-specific applications of WCF 21.7 and WLC Q116, and the expiry of the judicial law (WCF 19.4), cited only for the general permanence-of-the-law phrase, are not this passage's subject.*
