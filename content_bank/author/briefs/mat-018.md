# Theological Brief — MAT-018: Adultery (Matthew 5:27-30)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus, the Lawgiver interpreting His own law, deepens the seventh commandment past the outward act to the heart. "You have heard... Do not commit adultery," but He says the man who "looks at a woman to lust after her has already committed adultery with her in his heart" (v27-28). The command reaches desire, not merely deed. He then presses the cost of resisting it: if the right eye or right hand "causes you to sin," it is better to lose that member than for "your whole body to be thrown into hell" (v29-30). The stress falls on inward purity and radical, self-denying mortification.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *"Looketh... to lust after her"* — MHC and JFB: not only a married man's wife but any woman; the settled intent and consent of the will, the eye as "inlet and outlet" of this sin. *"Right eye / right hand"* — the dearer, more useful member; JFB: not the bodily organ literally, but the offending eye and hand as occasions of sin, striking at the root of unholy disposition. *"Pluck it out... cut it off"* — indignant promptitude; MHC reads it as mortification and self-denial. *"Hell"* — MHC: Christ Himself preaches damnation; some sins must be fled with fear.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Treating this text as inspired and Scripture interpreting Scripture (WCF 1.9-1.10), the divines ground the seventh commandment's spiritual reach here. WLC Q139 (via v28) lists among sins forbidden "all unclean imaginations, thoughts, purposes, and affections... wanton looks." WLC Q99 supplies the reading rule (via v27-28): the law "is spiritual, and so reaches the understanding, will, affections," forbidding "the least degree of every sin," including its "causes, means, occasions."
