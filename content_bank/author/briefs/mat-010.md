# Theological brief — MAT-010: Jesus Begins His Ministry (MAT.4.12–17)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** When John is imprisoned, Jesus withdraws to Galilee, leaves Nazareth, and settles in Capernaum by the sea. The evangelist stresses that this move *fulfills* Isaiah: the very region long "in darkness" and "the shadow of death" now sees "a great light." From that time Jesus begins to preach one summons — "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near" — the same word John had preached. The passage marks the deliberate, gradual opening of Christ's public Galilean ministry, framed as prophecy fulfilled and light dawning.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Withdrew* — literally "was delivered up" of John; Jesus takes note of the saints' sufferings and steers his course by them (JFB, MHC). *Great light* — the clear, growing gospel-revelation, day dawning on those benighted, "sprung up" upon them before they sought it (MHC). *Sitting in darkness* — a settled, contented, endangered posture on the borders of death (MHC). *Kingdom of heaven is near* — plain, practical gospel; its economy only "at hand" until the cross and Pentecost (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** By the method of WCF 1.9–1.10, Matthew reads Isaiah 9 as one Author's single sense fulfilled in Christ; JFB affirms that if not fulfilled thus, it was fulfilled in no age. WLC Q48 (cited via MAT.4.1–12) reads v.12 within Christ's humbling in his life — subjecting himself and conflicting with the world's indignities (here, John's imprisonment and Nazareth's rejection).
