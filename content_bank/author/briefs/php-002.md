# PHP-002 — Christ Magnified Through Paul's Imprisonment (Philippians 1:12-26)

**Passage's own emphasis.** Paul reassures the Philippians that his imprisonment has not hindered but *advanced* the gospel: his chains are now known throughout the palace guard as suffered "for Christ," and most of the brothers, emboldened by his example, speak the word more fearlessly. Even rivals who preach from envy cannot rob his joy, because whatever the motive, "Christ is preached." His single aim is that Christ be exalted in his body, whether by life or death — for "to live is Christ, and to die is gain." Torn between departing to be with Christ (far better) and remaining for the church's progress, he is confident he will remain for their joy in the faith.

**Key terms.** *My chains / bonds* — endured openly "in Christ," so that Providence extracts good from evil (MHC). *Magnified / exalted* — Christ, not Paul, made great in the body, whether by living or dying. *To live is Christ* — Christ the principle, rule, and end of life (MHC). *To die is gain* — the state after death, "far better," being with Christ. *In a strait* — perplexed between two goods, not two evils.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired and sufficient, Scripture interpreting Scripture (WCF 1). WLC Q159 (via 1:15-17): the word is to be preached *sincerely, aiming at God's glory* — the standard the envious preachers fail. WCF 16.7 (via 1:15-16): works from an impure heart, though preaching Christ, "proceed not from a heart purified by faith" and cannot please God — yet God still uses the proclamation. WCF 32.1 and WLC Q85-86 (via 1:23): at death the righteous soul, "made perfect in holiness," is "received into the highest heavens" — the ground of "with Christ, which is far better."

**Cross-references.** GAL.2.20 — "to live is Christ" as Christ living in me. 2CO.5.8 — absent from the body, present with the Lord. 2CO.5.1 — the eternal house not made with hands. COL.3.4 — Christ our life, appearing in glory. REV.14.13 — blessed dead who die in the Lord. ROM.8.28 — good brought from adverse circumstance (1:12). 2TI.2.9 — "the word of God is not bound" (1:12). LUK.9.50 / MRK.9.38-40 — rejoicing that Christ is preached even by outsiders (1:18).

**Reading moves.** Epistle: D7 = trace the argument — "actually served" (1:12), "what then?" (1:18), "for" (1:21) — and the strait resolving to a settled confidence (1:24-25). D3 on the load-bearing terms *bonds*, *magnified*, *gain*, noting the shift from Paul's circumstances (12-18) to his own life-or-death indifference (20-26).
