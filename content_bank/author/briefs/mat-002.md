# Theological Brief — MAT-002: The Birth of Jesus (MAT.1.18-25)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** The text announces how the birth of Jesus Christ "came about": the child conceived in Mary is "of the Holy Spirit," not by ordinary generation. The narrative turns on Joseph — a righteous man who, believing the worst, still refuses to disgrace Mary and resolves to divorce her quietly — until the angel redirects him. The passage stresses two God-given names that carry its whole freight: Jesus, "because He will save His people from their sins," and Immanuel, "God with us." It closes with Joseph's prompt, complete obedience.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Espoused / betrothed* — a binding contract, not yet full marriage (JFB: "valid marriage"). *A just man* — strict toward the law yet inclined to mercy, unwilling to expose her (MHC). *Jesus* — "Jehovah the Saviour," saving from the guilt and dominion of sin, not in sin but from it. *Immanuel* — "God with us," God manifested in the flesh, reconciling God and man; fulfilled in intent by the name Jesus, "for if he had not been Immanuel... he could not have been Jesus."

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1.9): the passage reads Isaiah 7:14 as one determinate sense fulfilled here. Doctrine: WLC Q41 (via v21) — He is called Jesus "because he saveth his people from their sins"; WLC Q40 (via v21) — the Mediator is God and man in one person, that each nature's works avail for us. WCF 24.5 (via vv18-20) supplies only its reading of betrothal as a binding contract.

**Cross-references.** ISA.7.14 — the virgin-Immanuel sign fulfilled. ISA.9.6-7 — the promised Davidic Son. ACT.13.23 — Savior from David's line. ACT.4.12 — salvation in no other name. JHN.1.29 — takes away sin. 1JN.3.5 — manifested to take away sins. GAL.4.4-5 — born of woman in the fullness of time.

**Reading moves.** D3 on the two named terms — *Jesus* and *Immanuel* — whose commentary-given senses (Saviour; God with us) carry the passage's doctrine. D7 = trace the fulfillment formula (v22 "to fulfill what the Lord had said"): prophecy → fulfillment, the one sense of Isaiah 7:14 realized in this birth; otherwise straightforward narrative.

*Off-agenda (set aside): WCF 24.5's provisions on divorce for adultery and remarriage, and the perpetual-virginity dispute the commentary itself leaves undecided.*
