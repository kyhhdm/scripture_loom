# PHP-009 — Final Exhortations to Joy, Peace, and Right Thinking (PHP.4.1-9)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Paul closes with rapid-fire exhortations to a beloved church. The governing charge is *stand firm in the Lord* (v1); from it flow the rest: two named women are urged *to agree in the Lord* and the church to help them (vv2-3); *rejoice in the Lord always* (v4); let *gentleness* be evident, for *the Lord is near* (v5); be *anxious for nothing* but bring *everything* to God in prayer *with thanksgiving* (v6), and the *peace of God* will *guard* heart and mind (v7); finally, *think on* whatever is true, honorable, right, pure, lovely, admirable (v8), and *put into practice* what they learned and saw in Paul (v9). The section moves from standing firm, through joy and prayer, to disciplined thinking and doing.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Stand fast* — steady and constant to the end, in Christ's strength, not our own (MHC). *Moderation/gentleness* — yieldingness, considerateness that does not press one's rights to the uttermost (JFB). *Be careful for nothing* — avoid the anxious, distrustful care that distracts, not the diligent care that is duty (MHC). *Guard* — keep as a well-garrisoned stronghold (JFB). *Think on* — have continual regard so as to *do* (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired and sufficient (WCF 1), the text's plain sense governs. On v6, prayer with thanksgiving is a special part of worship, required of all, offered in Christ's name by the Spirit's help (WCF 21.3; WLC Q178, Q185); it belongs to rejoicing in and trusting God (WLC Q104). On v8, the ninth commandment requires studying and practicing whatever is true, honest, lovely, and of good report (WLC Q144).

PHP.4.7 -> JHN.14.27 (Christ's peace); ISA.26.3 (kept in perfect peace, mind stayed on God). PHP.4.8 -> JAS.3.17 (wisdom pure, peaceable); TIT.2.7 (a pattern of good works, like Paul's example, v9).

**Reading moves.** Epistle: D7 = trace the *therefore* from ch.3 into v1, then follow the imperative chain (stand -> rejoice -> pray -> think -> do), noting the *Lord is near / God of peace* frame at vv5,9. D3 on the argument's key terms — gentleness, anxious care, guard, think on.
