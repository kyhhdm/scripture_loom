# Theological brief — MAT-027: Do Not Worry (MAT.6.25-34)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus commands his disciples, thrice over ("do not worry," vv. 25, 31, 34), not to be consumed with anxious care about food, drink, and clothing. He argues from the greater to the less: life is more than food, the body more than clothes (v. 25); the Father feeds the birds and clothes the grass, so how much more will he provide for his children, who are of far greater value (vv. 26, 30). Worry adds nothing (v. 27); such striving marks the Gentiles, who do not know the Father who already knows their needs (v. 32). The command's positive center is v. 33: "seek first the kingdom of God and his righteousness," and the necessary things "will be added." Tomorrow's cares belong to tomorrow (v. 34).

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Do not worry" / "take no thought" — anxious, distrustful solicitude springing from unbelief, not the lawful, prudent forethought Scripture requires (JFB, MHC; the old English "thought" meant "solicitude"). "O you of little faith" — JFB reads this as gently chiding the spirit of unbelief natural even to the best, not rebuking a settled failure; MHC: inordinate care exposes weak faith. "Add a single hour" (v. 27) — JFB judges the primary sense to be length of life's span, not bodily height. "Seek first the kingdom... and his righteousness" — heaven as the grand end, holiness as the way, made the supreme pursuit above all else.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive this as the inspired, sufficient words of Christ, read by the light of clearer Scripture. Doctrine: WCF 12.1 (via v. 30) grounds the fatherly-provision note — the adopted are "pitied, protected, provided for... by him as by a father"; WCF 14.3 (via v. 30) reads "little faith" as faith that, though weak, "gets the victory." WLC Q74 (via v. 32) sets believers "under his fatherly care and dispensations." WLC Q136 (via v. 31) and Q142 (via v. 25) name "distracting cares" and "distrustful and distracting cares" as sin — the passage's own indictment of worry.

**Cross-references.** MAT.6.33→LUK.12.31 (parallel: seek the kingdom); →MAT.5.6 (hunger for righteousness); →JHN.6.27 (labor for enduring food); →PSA.34.9-10; PSA.84.11-12; PSA.37.3, 37.18-19, 37.25 (the LORD provides for those who fear/trust him, no good withheld); →MRK.10.29-30; MAT.19.29 (the kingdom's "added" reward); →ROM.14.17 (kingdom = righteousness, not food/drink); →PRO.3.9-10; PRO.2.1-9; 2CH.31.20-21 (seeking God brings supply); →ROM.8.31 (the Father who is for us).

**Reading moves.** Genre: dominical wisdom-discourse in the Sermon on the Mount, dense with nature imagery. D7 = image → referent, argued lesser-to-greater (birds fed, lilies clothed → your heavenly Father provides for you); trace each "therefore" and note the pivot at v. 33 where prohibition turns to positive command. D3: fix "take no thought / do not worry" as anxious care, not forethought — a term whose sense has shifted since 1611.

Off-agenda, set aside: the sixth-commandment items in WLC Q136 (killing, war, self-defense) and the eighth-commandment catalog in WLC Q142 (theft, fraud, usury) contribute here only their naming of anxious, distracting cares.
