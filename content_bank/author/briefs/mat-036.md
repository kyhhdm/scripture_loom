# Theological Brief — MAT-036: Jesus Heals at Peter's House (Matthew 8:14–17)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** This passage shows Christ's power and compassion healing bodies. He heals one household member (Peter's mother-in-law), then, that evening, drives out spirits "with a word" and heals "all the sick." Matthew closes by declaring the whole scene a fulfillment of Isaiah: this is who the Messiah is, the one who "took on our infirmities and carried our diseases." The stress falls on Christ's authority over sickness and demons, and on the prophetic word being accomplished in Him.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Touched her hand" — not diagnosis, but the tender exertion of Christ's healing power. "Began to serve them" — the completed, sanctified mercy: healed immediately and fully, she rises at once to minister. "With a word" — dispossessing Satan, whose works Christ came to break. "Bore our infirmities" — Christ bore sickness by the miracles of His life, as He bore sin by His death; sin is the cause and sting of sickness.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired, sufficient, self-interpreting Scripture (WCF 1). No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; add no doctrine the lampposts do not support.

**Cross-references.** ISA.53.4 — the prophecy Matthew names fulfilled; 1PE.2.24 — same Isaiah text, construed of bearing sin; EXO.15.26 — the LORD who heals; MAT.1.22 — Matthew's fulfillment pattern; LUK.4.38-41 / MRK.1.29-34 — parallel accounts of this evening.

**Reading moves.** Standard narrative; D7 note the fulfillment citation at v17 (event -> prophecy).
