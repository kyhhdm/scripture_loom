# Theological Brief — MAT-005: Weeping and Great Mourning (MAT.2.16-18)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Herod, having been outwitted by the Magi, is "filled with rage" and slaughters the boys of Bethlehem and its vicinity, two years old and under, calculating the age from what the Magi told him. The text then declares this a fulfillment: "Then what was spoken through the prophet Jeremiah was fulfilled." Its stress falls on two things held together — a tyrant's cruelty against the infant King who has already escaped his reach, and the Word that foresaw and encompasses even this grief. The mourning of Ramah is real and inconsolable, yet it stands inside the sure fulfillment of prophecy.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Filled with rage / exceeding wroth" — inveterate corruption swelling higher for being disappointed (MHC); Herod, mocked not by the Magi but by God who has him in derision (JFB). "Fulfilled" — the prophecy accomplished first in Jeremiah's day at Ramah's captivity, and now again here, "the fulness of the scripture" (MHC). "Rachel weeping... refusing to be comforted" — Rachel, buried near Bethlehem, figuratively rising to lament her children; the degree of grief is "lamentation, and mourning, and great mourning," yet these babes are "not lost, but gone before" (MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** By WCF ch.1 the text is received as the inspired Word (1.4), and its own citation of Jeremiah is Scripture interpreting Scripture (1.9) — the one true sense searched by the clearer place. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; add no doctrine the lampposts do not support.

**Reading moves (per dimension).** D7 = trace the "then... was fulfilled" — narrative event to prophetic referent, and note the double horizon within v18 (Jeremiah's captivity and Bethlehem's slaughter). D3 on "fulfilled," "great mourning," "refusing to be comforted."

Off-agenda, set aside: traditions on the number of the slain, and Herod's other domestic murders.
