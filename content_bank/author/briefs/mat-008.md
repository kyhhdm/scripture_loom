# Theological brief — MAT-008: The Baptism of Jesus (MAT.3.13-17)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus comes from Galilee to the Jordan to be baptized by John. John recoils — the servant would sooner be baptized by the Master — but Jesus insists: "It is fitting for us to fulfill all righteousness in this way." The center is Christ's willing humility (he who knew no sin submitting to a baptism of repentance) and heaven's public answer to it: the heavens open, the Spirit descends and rests on him like a dove, and the Father's voice names him "My beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased." Christ's self-abasement is met by Father, Son, and Spirit together attesting who he is as he enters his public work.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Fulfill all righteousness* — Christ owns every divine institution and impledges himself to the whole righteousness of the Law (JFB, MHC). *Fitting/becometh* — not merely necessary but graceful and proper to him. *Descending... resting* — the Spirit does not merely touch but abides on him permanently, in his official capacity (JFB on ISA.11.2; JHN.1.32-34). *Beloved Son* — Son by eternal generation and by designation to the Redeemer's office; the aorist marks an absolute, settled complacency (JFB). *Like a dove* — harmless, pure, gentle (MHC, JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Reading the text as inspired and Scripture-interpreting-Scripture (WCF 1.9): the three Persons together — Father's voice, Son baptized, Spirit descending — display the one Godhead in three Persons (WCF 2.3; WLC Q9, cited via MAT.3.16-17). The Mediator willingly undertakes his office (WCF 8.4, via v15). That the Father is "well pleased" is grounded partly here in Christ's accepted obedience (WCF 11.3; via v17) — used only for its reading of the Father's pleasure resting on the obedient Son.

**Cross-references.** ISA.42.1 — the Servant God upholds, his Elect in whom his soul delights, echoed in the voice. PSA.2.7 — "You are my Son." MAT.17.5 — the same Father's word at the Transfiguration. ISA.11.2 — the Spirit resting on Messiah. JHN.1.31-34 — John's testimony to what he saw. 2PE.1.17 — the voice recalled as apostolic witness.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** Narrative, but not flat: D7 = trace the exchange between John and Jesus, then read the theophany (opened heavens, dove, voice) as the Father's answer interpreting the act. D3 on *fulfill all righteousness* and *beloved Son* — carry the commentary's sense, not a sharpened one.

Off-agenda: WLC Q38 and Q40 (why the Mediator must be God / God and man in one person) and WCF 11.3's fuller doctrine of justification's free grace are set aside; only their bearing on the Father's declared pleasure and the Son's identity is used here.
