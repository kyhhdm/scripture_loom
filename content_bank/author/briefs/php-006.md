# Stage-1 Theological Brief — PHP-006: Timothy and Epaphroditus Commended (PHP.2.19-30)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Paul commends two proven fellow workers whom he is sending to Philippi. Timothy is unique — he "genuinely" cares for the Philippians' state, unlike "all the others" who seek their own interests, not those of Jesus Christ (2.20-21). His worth is *proven*: as a child serves a father, he served with Paul for the gospel (2.22). Epaphroditus — brother, fellow worker, fellow soldier, their messenger and minister — nearly died "for the work of Christ," so the church must welcome him with joy and honor such men (2.25-30). The stress falls on tested, self-forgetful, Christ-seeking service that the church should recognize and prize.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Genuinely / naturally care* — MHC: "sincerely, and not in pretence only," duty become "in a manner natural"; JFB: "like-souled," Paul's second self. *Proven worth* — MHC: a man "tried," who "made full proof of his ministry." *Seek their own* (2.21) — MHC reads it as the generality, "all in comparison of Timothy," not literally every minister; JFB notes some at Rome were genuine, just not so self-sacrificing. *Fellow soldier* — JFB: in "the good fight of faith." *Nearly unto death... God had mercy* (2.27) — JFB: apostles lacked a permanent healing gift; mercy spared Paul "sorrow upon sorrow."

**Doctrinal anchors.** Reading by WCF ch.1: Scripture inspired, sufficient, self-interpreting (1.9-10). WLC Q105 (via 2.21) names "self-love, self-seeking... inordinate... setting of our mind, will, or affections upon other things" as sin against the first commandment — the very fault Timothy lacks. WLC Q130 (via 2.21), on superiors' sins, warns against "an inordinate seeking of themselves, their own glory, ease, profit, or pleasure" — the leaders' foil to Timothy's model. Use only this self-seeking strand; the catechisms' fuller lists lie off-agenda.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** Epistle. D7 = trace the *for/therefore* logic — why Timothy above all (2.20-21), why send Epaphroditus now (2.26-28) — and the contrast structure (Timothy/"all the others"). D3 on the argument's key terms: *genuinely care*, *proven worth*, *seek their own*, *fellow soldier*.

*Set aside as off-agenda:* WLC Q105/Q130's broader catalogues of first-commandment and superiors' sins beyond the self-seeking clause; Epaphroditus's church office (JFB's presbyter/deacon conjecture).
