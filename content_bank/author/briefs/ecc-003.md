# Theological Brief — ECC-003: The Vanity of Pleasure (ECC.2.1-11)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Solomon narrates a deliberate experiment: he "tests" himself with pleasure to see what is truly worthwhile for men "under heaven" during their few days. He runs through mirth and laughter, then wine, then vast works — houses, vineyards, gardens, parks, reservoirs, servants, herds, silver and gold, singers, every delight his eyes desired — all pursued "for myself." Crucially, his wisdom "remained with him" throughout; this is a controlled trial, not a drunkard's slide. The verdict, delivered on review (v11), is total: everything was futile, a chasing after wind, with nothing to be gained under the sun.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Vanity / futile* — yields no solid satisfaction, no true content (MHC). *Laughter is folly / "mad"* — harmless in its place, but empty when made the chief good (JFB). *"Under heaven / under the sun"* — the horizon of this-worldly labor and enjoyment. *"My wisdom remained with me"* — Solomon stayed master of his pleasures, a spy in the enemy's country, not their slave, and so a competent judge of them (MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive this as inspired and sufficient, and let Scripture interpret Scripture. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrinal anchor is asserted; the text stands on its own plain sense — created goods, sought as the highest good, cannot satisfy the soul.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** Wisdom-literature autobiography: D7 = read the accumulating "I built / I made / I gathered" catalog as the case-building toward the review verdict in v11; note the turn from the sensuous enumeration to the retrospective judgment ("Yet when I considered…"). D3 = weigh the loaded terms *vanity*, *under the sun*, *pursuit of the wind*, and *folly* in the commentary's controlled sense, not as blanket condemnation of the goods themselves.
