# Theological brief — MAT-049: Sheep among Wolves (MAT.10.16-25)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus sends the disciples out "like sheep among wolves," forewarning them that their mission means suffering, not pomp. They will be handed to councils, flogged in synagogues, dragged before governors and kings, betrayed by their own kin, and hated by everyone for His name. Yet the warning is braced by promise: in the hour of trial the Spirit of the Father speaks through them; the one who perseveres to the end is saved; and their lot is simply their Teacher's own — if the master of the house was called Beelzebul, the household should expect no better.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Sheep among wolves* — defenseless disciples exposed to men "ready to make a prey" of them (JFB), wolves being those whose nature is to devour (MHC). *Wise as serpents, harmless as doves* — a combination: serpent-wisdom that avoids needless exposure without sinful expedients to escape; dove-harmlessness that returns no injury (JFB, MHC). *Spirit of your Father* — "it is not ye that speak"; God's own assistance given "in that same hour." *Endureth to the end* — perseverance as the evidence of a real profession, against "drawing back" (JFB). *Beelzebul* — the most opprobrious slander; if the head is so named, "how much more the members of his household."

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): receive the passage as inspired and sufficient, letting Scripture interpret Scripture. The Spirit's speaking (v.20) is His illumination and help, not new revelation added to the written Word (cf. WCF 1.6). No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrine is asserted beyond the text's own emphasis.

**Cross-references.**
- MAT.10.22 -> MAT.24.13 — the same "endure to the end shall be saved," reiterated over Jerusalem.
- MAT.10.22 -> JAS.1.12 — the crown of life for the one who perseveres.
- MAT.10.22 -> REV.2.10 — be faithful unto death.
- MAT.10.16 -> LUK.10.3 — parallel sending, "as lambs among wolves."
- MAT.10.16 -> PHP.2.15 — blameless and innocent in a crooked generation.
- MAT.10.20 -> ACT.6.10 — Spirit-given words the enemies could not resist.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** This is Jesus' sending discourse (predictive warning), dense with metaphor. D3 = the figures themselves (sheep/wolves, serpents/doves, Beelzebul/household). D7 = image -> referent (sheep = defenseless disciples, wolves = persecutors); note the shift at v.24 from *predicting* suffering to the disciple-teacher principle that *grounds* it.

Set aside as off-agenda: the disputed timing of "the Son of Man comes" (v.23), which the lampposts flag as interpretively contested.
