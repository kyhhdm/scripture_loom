# Theological brief — ECC-012: The Value and Scarcity of Wisdom (ECC.7.11-29)

**Passage's own emphasis.** The passage praises wisdom, then exposes its limit and man's sin. Wisdom, like an inheritance and like money, is a shelter that preserves its owner (7:11-12,19); yet no one can straighten what God has bent (7:13). God makes both prosperity and adversity, so a man cannot discover what comes after him (7:14). Fearing God holds together two warnings — against being "overly righteous"/"too wise" and against being "excessively wicked" (7:16-18). The turn comes at 7:20: "there is no righteous man on earth who does good and never sins." Solomon resolved, "I will be wise," but it was beyond him (7:23-24). His verdict: "God made men upright, but they have sought out many schemes" (7:29).

**Key terms.** "Righteous over much" (JFB): a self-made, pharisaical righteousness separated from God, not true piety — hence the "fear of God" set against it. "Shelter/shadow" (MHC): wisdom, like money, defends against life's storms. "Upright" (MHC): man as first made, wholly determined to God. "Many schemes/inventions" (MHC): man's turning from resting in God to his own devices.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Scripture interprets Scripture (WCF 1.9): 7:20 grounds that corruption remains even in the regenerate (WCF 6.5) and that no man can perfectly keep God's commandments but daily breaks them in thought, word, and deed (WLC Q149). 7:29 grounds that God made man upright, endued with knowledge, righteousness, and holiness (WCF 4.2; WLC Q17), yet mutably able to fall (WCF 9.2; WLC Q21), so that by seeking his own inventions man fell from original righteousness into sin (WCF 6.2).

**Cross-references.**
- 7:20 -> ROM.3.23 — all have sinned and fall short.
- 7:20 -> 1JN.1.8-10 — if we say we have no sin, we deceive ourselves.
- 7:20 -> PRO.20.9 — who can say, "I have kept my heart pure"?
- 7:16 -> ROM.12.3 — do not think of yourself more highly than you ought.
- 7:14 -> ECC.3.4 — a time to weep and a time to laugh; both are God's ordering.

**Reading moves.** Wisdom literature. D3: the loaded senses matter — "righteous over much" is not true righteousness, "fear of God" (7:18) is the interpretive hinge, "upright" vs. "schemes" (7:29) frames man's created state against his fall. D7: read the proverbial contrasts as such, not as systematic law; note the shift at 7:20/7:23 from wisdom's praise to wisdom's limit and man's universal sinfulness, and let the fear of God govern the paired warnings rather than license mediocrity.

Off-agenda (set aside): WCF 4.2's dominion and tree-of-knowledge clauses and WCF 19.1's covenant-of-works detail reach beyond this passage; used only for "God made man upright." Per JFB, 7:26's "snare" woman reflects Solomon's own bitter experience, not an unqualified verdict on women.
