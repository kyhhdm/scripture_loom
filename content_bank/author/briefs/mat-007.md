# Theological brief — MAT-007: The Mission of John the Baptist (MAT 3:1-12)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** John comes preaching one summons — "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near" — because a mightier One is coming. His whole mission is preparation: he is the forerunner who levels obstacles so the Lord's way is made straight. The passage presses that repentance must be real, evidenced by *fruit*; descent from Abraham secures nothing. Two destinies stand at the threshold: the coming One baptizes with the Holy Spirit and fire, gathering wheat and burning chaff.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Repent* — "bethink yourselves," a change of mind that produces a change of way (MHC); here it is the sense of sin that makes one flee the coming wrath (JFB). *Kingdom of heaven* — a spiritual kingdom, essentially so, for which repentance is the fitting preparation (JFB). *Fruit meet for repentance* — the reformation that befits a true penitent, not mere profession. *Baptize with the Holy Spirit and fire* — the same purifying Spirit-work under water and fire as twin emblems (JFB), not a separate hell-baptism.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired and sufficient, the text is interpreted by clearer Scripture (WCF 1.9): John *is* the voice Isaiah foretold. WLC Q153 grounds here what God requires to escape wrath — repentance toward God and faith in Christ — matching John's call and warning. On v11, WCF 27.3 supplies only its fitting note: the sign's efficacy rests on the Spirit's work, not the administrator — "I baptize with water; He baptizes with the Spirit."

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** D3: define *repentance* and *kingdom of heaven* in the text's own terms before the prophetic imagery. D7 = image → referent for the harvest metaphors (axe/root, winnowing fan, wheat/chaff, unquenchable fire); note the shift at v11-12 from John's ministry to the coming One's judgment.

Off-agenda, set aside: WCF 28.2 and WLC Q163/Q177 (baptism's mode, parts, and its distinction from the Lord's Supper) — cited via v11 for the sacrament generally, but beyond this passage's emphasis on John's preparatory water-baptism versus Christ's Spirit-baptism.
