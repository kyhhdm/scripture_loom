# Theological Brief — MAT-003: The Pilgrimage of the Magi (MAT.2.1-12)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Gentile Magi from the east, guided by a star, come to Jerusalem seeking "the One who has been born King of the Jews," "to worship Him." The narrative sets their eager seeking against the trouble of Herod and "all Jerusalem," and against the informed but unmoved chief priests and scribes, who name Bethlehem from prophecy yet do not go. Reaching the Child, the Magi fall down, worship, and present gold, frankincense, and myrrh; warned in a dream, they withdraw another way. The text stresses right response to Christ the King: those far off seek and worship; those near know yet stay away.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "King of the Jews" — the Messiah, born a King, ruler of spiritual Israel (MHC, JFB). "Worship" — not civil homage to a petty king but "lofty spiritual homage," religious offering to God (JFB on v11). "Troubled" — Herod fearing a rival; Jerusalem dreading upheaval, preferring present ease to Messiah's kingdom (MHC). "Shepherd/ruler" — kingly rule figured as pastoral care (JFB on v6).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1.9): Scripture interprets Scripture — the priests answer Herod straight from Micah, and the pack's links let prophecy read the event. Doctrine: WCF 23.3 and 31.2, cited via MAT.2.4-5, note only that a magistrate may lawfully convene the theologians of the nation to consult on matters of religion — as Herod here gathers chief priests and scribes; the confessions describe lawful use, not Herod's murderous motive.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** Standard narrative; D3/D7 well served. One shift: v5-6 embeds a prophetic quotation, so D7 briefly turns to prophecy-fulfillment (Micah read as pointing to this birth) before the narrative resumes.

*Off-agenda, set aside:* WCF 23.3/31.2's fuller content on the magistrate's duties and synods' powers is not taught by this passage and is not used.
