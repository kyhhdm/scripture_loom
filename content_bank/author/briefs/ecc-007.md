# Theological Brief — ECC-007: The Vanities of Oppression and Ambition (ECC.4.1-16)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** The Preacher "looks" and "sees" four griefs "under the sun": oppression where the oppressed have no comforter and power sits with oppressors (vv.1-3); labor driven by envy of one's neighbor, set against the fool's idleness, with a middle way of "one handful with tranquility" (vv.4-6); the solitary miser who toils endlessly yet bereaves his own soul of enjoyment (vv.7-8); and the vanity of even kingly succession, where the rising favorite is soon forgotten (vv.13-16). Against solitary futility stands the passage's positive center: "Two are better than one... a cord of three strands is not quickly broken" (vv.9-12).

**Key terms.** *No comforter* — the oppressed are slighted and left alone (MHC). *Envy of his neighbor* — prosperity/right work itself provokes the grudge (JFB: "prosperous"). *Eats his own flesh* — the idle fool is a self-tormentor, never satisfied (JFB). *Handful with tranquility* — mental quietness from honest labor, opposed both to anxious gain and to idleness. *Threefold cord* — union in marriage, friendship, and communion; where two are joined in love, Christ by his Spirit makes the third (MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** By WCF ch.1, this inspired and sufficient text is read by Scripture's own light. WLC Q142 (via ECC.4.8) names the eighth-commandment sins the passage exhibits: oppression, extortion, "envying at the prosperity of others," idleness, "distrustful and distracting cares... in getting, keeping, and using" goods, and "defrauding ourselves of the due use and comfort of that estate which God hath given us" — precisely the miser's self-bereavement. WLC Q151 (via ECC.4.13) supplies the aggravation fitting the "old but foolish king": sin against "public or private admonition," done "wilfully," by one whose office makes him a "guide to others"; use only this reading of admonition-refused, not the catechism's fuller catalogue.

**Cross-references.** PRO.27.17 — companions sharpen one another. GEN.2.18 — "not good that man should be alone," grounding vv.9-12. 1CO.12.18-21 — members needing one another. PRO.15.16-17 / PRO.16.8 / PSA.37.16 — the "little with quietness" of v.6. 1JN.2.16 — the covetous "eye" of v.8. 1JN.3.12 / ECC.3.16 — envy and oppression named earlier. 1TH.5.11 — mutual lifting up (v.10).

**Reading moves.** Wisdom reflection: D7 = trace the repeated observation ("I saw / I looked") to its verdict ("vanity and a pursuit of the wind"), and follow the "better... than" comparatives that carry the argument (vv.3,6,9,13). Watch the turn at v.9 from lament to counsel. D3 = fix the sense of "under the sun," "vanity/futile," and "comforter" as the commentary gives them.

Set aside as off-agenda: WLC Q151's aggravations from time/place, parties offended, and offenses beyond admonition-refused — cited here only for the foolish king's refusal of warning.
