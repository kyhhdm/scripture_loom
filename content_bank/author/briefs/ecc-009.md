# Theological Brief — ECC-009: The Vanity of Riches (ECC.5.9-20)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** The Preacher exposes hoarded wealth as futile. The produce of the earth is common to all, even the king (v.9); but the lover of money is never satisfied (v.10), gains only more mouths to feed and the pleasure of looking (v.11), loses sleep the laborer enjoys (v.12), and watches riches harm their owner, vanish in a failed venture, or leave him nothing to pass on (vv.13-14). He came naked and departs naked, carrying nothing, toiling for the wind (vv.15-16), eating his days in darkness, sorrow, sickness, and anger (v.17). Against this the passage sets what is "good and fitting": to eat, drink, and find satisfaction in one's labor as God's given lot — for the power to enjoy riches is itself a gift of God (vv.18-20).

**Key terms.** *Vanity / for the wind* — insubstantial, unsatisfying, uncertain (JFB, MHC). *Satisfied* — natural desires rest when met, but covetous desire is insatiable; silver feeds neither hunger nor soul (MHC on v.10). *Portion / lot* — the legitimate, God-given use of one's goods, not abuse (JFB v.18). *Gift of God* — not merely wealth but the power to eat thereof, wisdom and grace to enjoy and do good with it (MHC, JFB v.19).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Reading this inspired, sufficient text by Scripture interpreting Scripture (WCF 1.9): WLC Q142 (via v.12) names as sin "covetousness; inordinate prizing and affecting worldly goods; distrustful and distracting cares... in getting, keeping, and using them," and equally "defrauding ourselves of the due use and comfort of that estate which God hath given us" — both halves are this passage's twin warnings. WLC Q135 (via v.12) commends "a sober use of meat, drink, physic, sleep, labor," and "quietness of mind, cheerfulness of spirit" — the contentment the text calls a gift.

**Cross-references.** 1TI.6.10 (love of money a root of evil, v.10); MAT.6.24 (cannot serve God and mammon, v.10); LUK.12.15 (life not in abundance of possessions, v.10); MAT.6.19 (treasure not on earth, v.10); DEU.8.18 (God gives power to get wealth, v.19); JOB.1.21 / 1TI.6.7 (naked in, nothing out, v.15); PSA.49.17 (carries nothing at death, v.15); ECC.2.24 / ECC.3.12-13 / ECC.3.22 (eat, drink, enjoy labor as God's gift, vv.18-19); ECC.6.2 (the man denied power to enjoy, contrast, v.19); 2CH.1.12 (riches given by God, v.19).

**Reading moves.** Wisdom genre. D7 = trace the argument's turn: the accumulating "grievous evil" observations (vv.10-17) pivot at v.18's "Here is what I have seen to be good" into the enjoyment-as-gift resolution — read the two poles together, not the lament alone. D3 = hold the tension in the repeated key terms *satisfied*, *portion*, and *gift*, where the same object (riches) is vanity when hoarded and blessing when received from God's hand.

Set aside as off-agenda: WLC Q142's list beyond covetousness (theft, false weights, usury, etc.) and WLC Q135's fuller sixth-commandment duties (defense of life, reconciliation) — cited here only for their bearing on the sober use of goods and quietness of spirit.
