# Theological Brief — MAT-012: Jesus Heals the Multitudes (Matthew 4:23-25)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** This summary text stresses the twofold public ministry of Jesus across all Galilee: He *teaches* and *preaches the gospel of the kingdom*, and He *heals every disease and sickness among the people*. The reach is total — "all" and "every" recur — extending His fame throughout Syria and drawing large crowds from Galilee, the Decapolis, Jerusalem, Judea, and beyond the Jordan. Word and work go together; the healing accompanies and confirms the preaching.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "The gospel of the kingdom" — the glad tidings of the kingdom of grace and glory now come, of which Christ is Himself the Preacher (MHC). "Synagogues" — the places of religious concourse where Old Testament Scripture was read, natural ground for introducing the kingdom's gospel. "Healed them" — cures wrought immediately and by His word, which MHC reads as sign (proof of a Teacher come from God), mercy (blessing, not plague), and mystery (bodily cures signifying that His great errand was to heal spiritual maladies). "Various diseases," "demon-possessed," "seizures," "the paralyzed" — the greatest weaknesses of body and mind, all under His command.

**Doctrinal anchors.** By WCF 1.9, Scripture interprets Scripture: read this summary as the Spirit's inspired witness that Jesus is the promised Messiah whose works authenticate His word. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; add no doctrine the lampposts do not supply. Note MHC's own qualification — many who rejoiced in Christ the Healer later forgot Him as Teacher.
