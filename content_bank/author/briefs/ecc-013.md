# Theological brief — ECC-013: Wisdom in Obeying the King (ECC.8.1-17)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Qoheleth commends wisdom that brightens a man's face, then presses one instance of it: keep the king's command "because of your oath before God," not persisting in a bad cause, for a wise heart discerns the right time and procedure. Yet no one knows the future, and no one has power over the wind or the day of death. Though the wicked prosper, are honored, and judgment is delayed, Qoheleth still knows it will go well with those who fear God and not well with the wicked who do not fear Him. The mismatch of deserts "under the sun" is futile; so he commends thankful enjoyment of God's gifts and confesses that man cannot comprehend God's work.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Wisdom* — true piety guided by prudence, making a man's face shine (MHC). *Keep the king's command / oath of God* — subjection to authority for conscience' sake, an oath God witnesses (MHC); JFB reads the king as Jehovah, though the passage's plain counsel is lawful submission. *Time and judgment* — the wise heart discerns the fit season and manner, and reckons with God's appointed judgment (JFB). *No discharge in that war* — death admits no exemption (JFB). *Fear God, reverent in His presence* — the ground of it going well.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): treat the text as inspired and sufficient; its deferred-justice tension is resolved not by adding to it but by clearer Scripture on final judgment (1.9). Doctrine: WLC Q113 (via ECC.8.11) forbids "murmuring and quarreling at, curious prying into, and misapplying of God's decrees and providences" — precisely the abuse the delayed sentence tempts.

**Cross-references.** ECC.8.11→ROM.2.4-5 (forbearance meant to lead to repentance, not license); ECC.8.11→2PE.3.3-10 (the Lord's delay is not slackness); ECC.8.11→ISA.26.10 (favor shown the wicked, who still spurn righteousness); ECC.8.12→PSA.37.11 (well with those who fear God); ECC.8.8→JOB.14.5 (days determined, none escape death); ECC.8.6→ECC.3.1,3.17 (a time and judgment for every purpose); ECC.8.17→ECC.3.11 (man cannot find out God's work).

**Reading moves (D3/D7).** Wisdom literature: D7 = observation → Qoheleth's verdict ("this too is vanity"); trace the shift from political counsel (vv1-9) to the scandal of deferred justice (vv10-14) to commended joy and confessed limits (vv15-17). D3 on the refrains "under the sun," "time and judgment," "fear God," "vanity."

*Set aside as off-agenda:* JFB's identification of "the king" as Jehovah (the plain sense is submission to lawful authority under one's oath before God); and WLC Q113's clauses on oaths, vows, and blasphemy, which do not bear on ECC.8.11.
