# Theological Brief — MAT-050: Fear God Alone (Matthew 10:26-31)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Three times Jesus commands His sent disciples, "Do not be afraid" (vv. 26, 28, 31), stationing that repeated command as the passage's spine. The persecutor's reach is bounded: he can kill the body but cannot touch the soul (v. 28). Over against that limited power stands the One to be feared — God, who can destroy both soul and body in hell — and the same God who is the Father numbering every hair and attending every sparrow's fall. Fear is not abolished but redirected: from man to God, and that redirected fear frees fearless open proclamation (v. 27) and rests in the Father's valuing care (v. 31).

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Fear them not* — the duty and interest of disciples; "they who truly fear God need not fear man" (MHC). *Kill the body... not the soul* — the enemy's rage reaches its "utmost," no further; "the soul is the man" (MHC). *Fear him... able to destroy both soul and body in hell* — a decisive proof of a hell for body as well as soul (JFB); the fear of God is "a sovereign antidote against the fear of man" (MHC). *Not... apart from the will of your Father* / *hairs... numbered* — nothing befalls the smallest creature outside the Father's care (JFB).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired and self-consistent, Scripture interpreting Scripture. On v. 28, WCF 4.2 / WLC Q17 supply only their reading that God endued man with a *reasonable and immortal soul* — grounding why the persecutor "cannot kill the soul"; their wider account of creation is off-agenda here. On vv. 29-31, WCF 5.1 / WLC Q18 confess that God upholds and governs "all creatures, actions, and things, from the greatest even to the least" — the very providence the sparrow and the numbered hairs display.

**Cross-references.**
- LUK.12.4-5 — parallel: fear Him who can cast into hell.
- ISA.8.12-13 — do not fear what they fear; sanctify the LORD as your dread.
- 1PE.3.14 — blessed sufferers; do not fear their fear.
- JAS.4.12 — one Lawgiver able to save and destroy.
- ROM.8.35-39 — no persecution separates from God's love.
- ISA.51.12 — why fear a man who dies, forgetting your Maker?
- LUK.12.6-7 — parallel: sparrows, numbered hairs, of more value.
- REV.2.10 — do not fear coming suffering; be faithful.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** D7 = weigh the two fears the text sets side by side (man vs. God) and trace how each "do not be afraid" rests on the reason just given; note the shift at v. 29 from *warning* (destroy in hell) to *comfort* (the Father's care). D3 on *body/soul*, *fear*, *destroy* as the passage uses them.

Off-agenda, set aside: the broader creation narrative and dominion in WCF 4.2 / WLC Q17, and the sending/persecution material framing the wider discourse (MHC on vv. 16-25, 34-42) beyond this pericope's redirected-fear emphasis.
