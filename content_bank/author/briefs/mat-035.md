# Theological brief — MAT-035: The Faith of the Centurion (MAT.8.5-13)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** A Gentile centurion pleads for his paralyzed servant. When Jesus offers to come, the centurion answers that he is not worthy to have Him under his roof — "just say the word, and my servant will be healed" — reasoning from his own place under authority: he commands, and soldiers and servants obey. Jesus marvels, declaring He "has not found anyone in Israel with such great faith," and foretells many coming from east and west to the banquet while the sons of the kingdom are cast out. The healing follows the word at that very hour. The stress falls on faith that trusts Christ's authoritative word to act at a distance.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Not worthy* — humility that thinks meanly of self and highly of Christ; Matthew Henry notes the more humility, the more faith. *Say the word* — owning a divine power that commands nature as it once said "Let there be light"; saying and doing are not two things with Christ. *So great faith* — a trusting (not a working) faith Christ Himself wrought and now admires. *Sons of the kingdom* — those children by profession or birth who rest in that and are cast out.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive the text as God's inspired, sufficient Word, and let clearer Scripture interpret it. Doctrine: WCF 14.3 (cited via MAT.8.10) — saving faith differs in degrees, weak or strong, yet gets the victory; the centurion's is its strong form. WCF 23.2 (cited via MAT.8.9-10) — only its point that holding the soldier's office is lawful and no bar to godliness; a centurion may be eminently faithful.

**Reading moves.** Standard narrative; D3/D7 well served by the plain account. Optional D7 note: trace the centurion's argument-from-authority in v9 to his conclusion in v8 ("just say the word").

Set aside as off-agenda: WCF 23.2's clause on waging just war contributes nothing here beyond the lawfulness of the soldier's calling.
