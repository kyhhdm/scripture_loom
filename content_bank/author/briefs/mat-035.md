# Theological brief — MAT-035: The Faith of the Centurion (MAT.8.5–13)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** A Gentile centurion pleads for his paralyzed servant and, when Jesus offers to come, answers that he is "not worthy" — "just say the word, and my servant will be healed." Reasoning from his own place "under authority," he trusts that Christ can command healing at a distance by a word alone. Jesus marvels: "I have not found anyone in Israel with such great faith," announces that many will come "from the east and the west" to Abraham's banquet while "the sons of the kingdom" are cast out, and heals the servant that very hour. The stress falls on faith that honors Christ's word-authority, and on that faith found outside Israel.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Not worthy* — humility that has "mean thought of himself, and high thoughts of our Lord Jesus"; the more humility, the more faith. *Say the word* — owning "a divine power," for Christ is "the eternal Word," with whom saying and doing are not two things. *Under authority* — the centurion's command over soldiers pictures Christ's command over disease. *Great faith* — Christ "marvelled," admiring not greatness but grace. *Sons of the kingdom* — those who rest in birthright profession and are cast out.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Treated as inspired and sufficient, Scripture interprets Scripture. WCF 14.3 (via MAT.8.10): saving faith differs "in degrees, weak or strong," yet "gets the victory" — here shown strong and commended. WCF 23.2 (via MAT.8.9–10): only its recognition that a Christian may lawfully hold the magistrate's office bears here — grounding the centurion's lawful authority under which he serves; its clauses on war are off-agenda.

**Reading moves.** Standard narrative; D3/D7 well served by the plain account.
