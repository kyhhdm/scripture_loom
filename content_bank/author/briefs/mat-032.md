# Theological brief — MAT-032: The House on the Rock (MAT.7.24-27)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Closing the Sermon on the Mount, Jesus divides all His hearers into two, and only two, kinds: those who hear "these words of Mine" *and act on them*, and those who hear and *do not act*. The decisive difference is not hearing — both builders hear — but doing. Each builds a house (a hope, a life); a storm of rain, torrents, and wind beats identically on both; the foundation alone determines whether the house stands or falls. Wisdom is obedient hearing; folly is hearing without doing. The fall of the second house is total: "great was its collapse."

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Hears... and acts on them* — to hear Christ is not merely to give Him a hearing but to obey (MHC). *Rock* — "the rock of true discipleship, or genuine subjection to Christ" (JFB); MHC anchors it further in Christ Himself as the only foundation. *Sand* — "a loose foundation... of an empty profession and mere external services" (JFB). *The storm* — the trial that tests what our hope is bottomed on, whether tribulation now or death and judgment at the last (MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive these as the very words of Christ, inspired and sufficient, needing no addition — and let Scripture interpret Scripture. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrine is asserted beyond the passage's own: saving hearing issues in doing.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** This is a parable/simile. D7 = image -> referent: map wise/foolish builder, rock/sand, storm onto hearing-and-doing versus hearing-only, and the day of testing; note the pairing is exact except the one variable (the foundation). D3 = hold the sense of "hears... and acts on them" against "hears... and does not act" — the passage's whole weight rests on that single contrast.

*Off-agenda, set aside:* MHC's surrounding exposition of vv.21-23 (false prophets, "I never knew you") and vv.28-29 (Christ's authoritative teaching) frame this pericope but are outside MAT.7.24-27; not developed here.
