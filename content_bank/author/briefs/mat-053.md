# Theological Brief — MAT-053: The Reward of Service (MAT.10.40-42)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Closing His charge to the sent disciples, Jesus binds the welcome given His messengers to Himself and to the Father: "He who receives you receives Me, and he who receives Me receives the One who sent Me." An identification chain runs from receiver to disciple to Christ to God. Then a descending scale of recipients — prophet, righteous man, and lowest of all "one of these little ones" — with a matching promise: whatever is done for such, even a cup of cold water, "because he is My disciple," will never lose its reward. The stress is that the smallest service rendered for Christ's sake is seen and surely rewarded.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Receives* — entertains, welcomes (JFB); the ambassador principle, "Your authority is Mine, as Mine is My Father's." *In the name of a prophet* — for his office's sake and love to his Master. *Little ones* — their lowliness in spirit, little in an undiscerning world's eyes yet high in Heaven's esteem (JFB, drawn from Zech 13:7). *Cup of cold water* — the smallest service. *In the name of a disciple* — "because ye are Christ's" (Mark 9:41). JFB marks a descending climax — prophet, righteous man, little one — signifying that however low we stoop in serving Christ's own, love bearing His name is divinely owned and rewarded.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): receive these as Christ's own inspired, sufficient words; let Scripture interpret Scripture — the parallel at Mark 9:41 and Matt 25:40 fix the sense. No confessional proof-text cites this pericope, so no doctrinal-anchor claim is drawn.
