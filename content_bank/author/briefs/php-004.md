# Theological Brief — PHP-004: The Humility and Exaltation of Christ (PHP.2.1-11)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Paul presses the church toward like-mindedness and lowly-mindedness (vv.1-4): do nothing from selfish ambition or empty pride, but in humility count others more important and attend to their interests. The ground and pattern is Christ (v.5): existing in the form of God, He did not grasp at equality but emptied Himself, took a servant's form, humbled Himself to death—even the cross (vv.6-8). Therefore God exalted Him and gave the name above all names, that every knee bow and every tongue confess Him Lord, to the Father's glory (vv.9-11). The ethical appeal is anchored in the descent and ascent of Christ.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Like-minded / one accord" — one in affection, "united souls" (JFB). "Lowliness of mind" — the creature's sense of dependence on God, leveling all (JFB). "Form of God" — the outward self-manifesting glory taking His divine nature for granted; not emptied of Godhead but of that glory (JFB, Bengel). "Emptied Himself" — presupposes prior "plenitude of Godhead"; He bore Himself as if empty. "Form of a servant" — a man of mean estate, obedient to death. "Highly exalted" — "super-eminently exalted," His human nature chiefly.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Treating the text as inspired and self-interpreting: WCF 8.2 and WLC Q36 read v.6 as the eternal Son, of one substance and equal with the Father, who took man's nature—two distinct natures, one person. WLC Q46 reads vv.6-8 as the estate of humiliation ("emptying himself of his glory, took upon him the form of a servant"); WCF 8.4 and WLC Q49 read v.8 as His willing, obedient, cursed death on the cross. WLC Q54 reads v.9 as His exaltation as God-man to highest favor and power. WLC Q42 spans vv.8-11 (humiliation and exaltation). WLC Q131 reads vv.3-4 as the duty of equals: honoring one another, rejoicing in another's gifts as one's own.

**Reading moves (per dimension — especially D3/D7).** Epistle: D7 = trace the "therefore" of vv.1-5 into the "therefore" of v.9, watching the hinge at v.5 where exhortation turns to the Christ-pattern and back to command. D3 = the argument's key terms are relational ("form," "emptied," "servant," "exalted," "Lord"); read v.6's "form of God" against v.7's "form of a servant" as deliberate contrast.
