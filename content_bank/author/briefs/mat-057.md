# Theological Brief — MAT-057: Rest for the Weary (Matthew 11:25–30)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus praises the Father, "Lord of heaven and earth," for sovereignly hiding "these things" from the wise and learned and revealing them to little children — well-pleasing in the Father's sight. He then declares that all things are entrusted to Him, that Father and Son mutually and exclusively know each other, and that the Son reveals the Father to whom He chooses. On that authority He invites all who are weary and burdened to come to Him for rest, to take His yoke and learn from Him — for He is gentle and humble in heart — promising rest for souls, "for My yoke is easy and My burden is light."

**Key terms (from commentary).** "These things" — the great saving truths of the gospel (MHC, JFB). "Wise and learned / babes" — men proud of speculative or worldly shrewdness, versus babe-like men of unassuming docility (JFB). "Entrusted/delivered" — the whole administration of the kingdom of grace committed to the Son as Mediator, including unlimited power (JFB). "Weary and burdened" — chiefly the guilt and power of sin (MHC). "Yoke" — subjection to Christ as servant and subject; "easy" here means gracious, sweet, "lined with love" (MHC). "Rest" — release from sin's terror and dominion, rest in God (MHC).

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): receive as inspired and self-interpreting — v29's "rest for your souls" is drawn from Jeremiah 6:16, Scripture speaking in Scripture's own words. Doctrine: the hiding/revealing of vv25–26 is resolved into divine sovereignty — WCF 3.7 and WLC Q13 (cited via 11.25–26) confess that God, "according to the unsearchable counsel of his own will," extends or withholds favor for the praise of His glory; the commentary keeps this as adoring acquiescence, not despair. WLC Q172 (cited via 11.28) contributes only its reading of the invitation: the doubting soul who "unfeignedly desires to be found in Christ" may come.

**Cross-references.** MAT.11.28→MAT.11.29 (invitation completed by the yoke); →JHN.7.37 (come and drink, the thirsty); →JHN.6.37 (whoever comes is not cast out); →ISA.55.1-3 (come, all who thirst); →GAL.5.1 (freedom, not the yoke of bondage); →JER.6.16 (source of "rest for your souls"). **Reading moves (per dimension).** Gospel narrative carrying a wisdom-style invitation with sustained metaphor. D7 = image → referent (yoke = subjection to and learning from Christ; rest = the soul's rest from sin) — note the shift from thanksgiving-and-revelation (vv25–27) to open invitation (vv28–30). D3 on "babes," "yoke," "rest," and the paradox "easy/light."

*Off-agenda, set aside:* WLC Q172's Lord's-Supper subject matter (used only for its reading of the weak, doubting, seeking heart).
