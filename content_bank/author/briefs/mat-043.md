# Theological Brief — MAT-043: The Patches and the Wineskins (MAT.9.16-17)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** With two homely pictures — an unshrunk-cloth patch on an old garment, and new wine in old wineskins — Jesus explains why His disciples do not fast as John's disciples and the Pharisees do. Forcing the new upon the old only ruins both: the patch tears worse, the skins burst, the wine spills. The stress falls on fitness and timing: a season, a strength, a duty each in its place. New wine belongs in new wineskins, "and both are preserved."

**Key terms (from commentary).** *New cloth / new wine* — the harder, more intense duties (like severe fasting) that require a maturity young disciples do not yet have. *Old garment / old bottles* — beginners "taken immediately from their callings," not yet used to religious austerities. *Preserved* — Christ's gentle care that considers "the weakness and infirmity of young Christians," lest the profession miscarry.

**Doctrinal anchors.** No confessional proof-text cites this pericope; none is asserted. Read as WCF 1 directs — inspired, sufficient, and interpreted by clearer Scripture.

**Cross-references.** 1CO.3.1-2 — milk before meat for babes in Christ. JHN.16.12 — "I have yet many things to say, but ye cannot bear them now." PSA.125.3 — God does not overload the righteous. ISA.40.11 — the Shepherd gently leads those with young. 1CO.13.13 — love as the enduring frame.

**Reading moves.** D7 = image → referent (patch/wineskins → the fitting of duty to maturity). D3 on *new/old* and *preserved*.

Off-agenda, set aside: the fasting-and-bridegroom dispute of vv.14-15 (the passage's occasion, not these two verses' own emphasis).
