# Theological brief — ECC-005: A Time for Everything (ECC.3.1-15)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Every event and condition of human life has an appointed season, fixed unalterably by God's sovereign counsel (3:1). The twenty-eight paired opposites (3:2-8) show that we live in a world of ceaseless change we cannot control — the contraries each take their turn. From this the Preacher draws that toil yields no lasting profit (3:9); God has laid this burden on men to occupy them (3:10). Yet He "makes everything beautiful in its time" and has set eternity in the heart, though man cannot fathom His whole work (3:11). The settled good is therefore to rejoice, do good, eat, drink, and find satisfaction in labor — this is God's gift (3:12-13). God's work endures forever, unchangeable, so that men should fear Him (3:14-15).

**Key terms.** *Season / time* — a divinely fixed, unalterable appointment, not human contingency (MHC). *Beautiful in its time* — each thing becoming in its proper season, as cold suits winter. *Set eternity/the world in the heart* — the commentary holds this both ways: a God-given capacity to perceive His ordered work (JFB, Pemble), and the world crowding the heart so we miss it (Reynolds). *Gift of God* — grace to enjoy labor's good. *Fear Him* — the end God intends by the mystery of His providence.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF ch.1): receive the text as inspired and sufficient; let clearer Scripture interpret it. Doctrine: WLC Q135 (via 3:4) reads the weep/laugh, mourn/dance seasons as calling for "quietness of mind, cheerfulness of spirit," patient bearing of God's hand. WLC Q141 (via 3:12-13) reads "a time to get… to keep" as the eighth-commandment duty of "provident care and study to get, keep, use, and dispose" what is needful — lawful diligence in season, not covetous hoarding.

**Cross-references.** ECC.3.17 — God will judge in His set time. MAT.16.3 — discerning the season. ECC.7.14 — receive both prosperity and adversity from God. ROM.1.19-20 — creation reflects God's ordered wisdom (via "beautiful in its time"). ROM.11.33 / JOB.11.7 — His work unsearchable, past finding out. GEN.1.31 / DEU.32.4 — all He does is very good, perfect.

**Reading moves (D3/D7).** Wisdom poetry. D7 = read each paired opposite as an instance of one truth — the sovereign appointment behind all events — not as a moral endorsement of both terms; watch the shift at v9 where the catalogue turns to theological reflection (profit, beauty, gift, fear). D3 on *time/season* and *beautiful in its time*.

Set aside as off-agenda: the sixth- and eighth-commandment catechism duties beyond the contentment/stewardship senses noted; JFB's disputed rendering of 3:15 ("avengeth the persecuted man").
