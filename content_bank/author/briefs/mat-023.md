# Theological brief — MAT-023: The Lord's Prayer (Matthew 6:5-15)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus corrects two faults in prayer and then supplies a model. Against the hypocrites who pray to be *seen of men*, He commands secret prayer to "thy Father who seeth in secret"; against the pagans' *babbling* "many words," He grounds prayer in a Father who "knows what you need before you ask." The pattern itself moves from God's honor (name hallowed, kingdom come, will done) to our needs (bread, forgiveness, deliverance). The passage closes by singling out one petition: forgiveness received is bound to forgiveness given (vv. 14-15).

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Hypocrites* — here the scribes and Pharisees whose aim is human applause, so "they have their reward" already. *Vain repetitions* — not all repetition, but senseless, showy multiplying of words (Christ Himself repeated words in fervor). *Debts* — sins as an obligation to God's justice; *trespasses* names the same, an "extenuating" softer word for wrongs done to us. *Daily bread* — the bread of subsistence for this day, teaching childlike dependence.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired and sufficient, the text is Christ's own directory for prayer. WLC 186-187: the Lord's Prayer is both pattern *and* a prayer to be used, with understanding and faith. WLC 189 (v. 9): the preface teaches us to draw near with reverence and confidence of the Father's goodness, "with and for others." WLC 194 / WCF 11.5 (v. 12): we ask, through Christ's satisfaction apprehended by faith, pardon of daily failings — the justified may fall under God's *fatherly* displeasure, not from justification. WCF 21.3/21.6 (vv. 11-12): prayer is worship required of all, acceptable in Christ's name by the Spirit, tied to no place; God is worshipped everywhere in spirit and truth. WLC 112 (v. 9): the name — all whereby God makes Himself known — is to be held holy. WCF 16.7 (v. 5): the hypocrites' works, not proceeding from faith nor aimed at God's glory, cannot please Him.

**Reading moves (per dimension).** D7 = trace the two-part structure: reproof (vv. 5-8) governs the model (vv. 9-13), and the model's own descending-then-ascending order (God's honor first, then our needs) is load-bearing; note the deliberate return to one petition in vv. 14-15. D3 = the passage's key terms carry precise senses — press "debts/trespasses," "vain repetitions," and "daily" against the commentary rather than modern usage.
