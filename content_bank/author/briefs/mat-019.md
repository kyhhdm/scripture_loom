# Theological brief — MAT-019: Divorce (MAT.5.31-32)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Continuing His exposition of the seventh commandment, Christ takes up the permission of divorce. He cites the concession that a man putting away his wife must give her "a certificate of divorce," then reasserts marriage's binding character: to divorce "except for sexual immorality" is to bring adultery upon her, and to marry a divorced woman is to commit adultery. The stress falls on protecting the marriage bond and exposing how a lax reading of the concession opened a door to adultery.

**Key terms (from commentary).** "Certificate of divorce" — a legal, deliberate instrument, not a word spoken in passion; the law's original intent was to restrain "rash and hasty divorces" (MHC), a "legal check upon reckless and tyrannical separation" (JFB). "Except for sexual immorality" — the one legitimate ground, conjugal infidelity, which "breaks the marriage covenant" (MHC); the concession was later stretched to permit divorce "for any cause" (MHC), the lax Hillel view Christ corrects.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Read as inspired and interpreting itself (WCF 1.9): Christ, the law's own Author, gives its strict sense. WLC Q139 names "unjust divorce" among sins forbidden in the seventh commandment — the doctrine here. WCF 24.5 (cited via 5.31-32) grants that only in the case of adultery may the innocent party lawfully sue for divorce and remarry; note JFB's caution that remarriage of the innocent party "is not treated of here."

**Reading moves.** D7 = trace the concession-to-correction move: "It hath been said… but I tell you" reframes a permission (not a command) against marriage's institution. D3 on "certificate of divorce" and "except for sexual immorality" — the two terms that carry the whole reversal.
