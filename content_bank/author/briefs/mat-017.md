# Theological brief — MAT-017: Anger and Reconciliation (Matthew 5:21–26)

**Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus expounds the sixth commandment against its narrowed traditional gloss. "You have heard... but I tell you": the true reach of "Do not murder" condemns not only the killing act but the anger, contempt, and reviling words behind it — heart and tongue, not merely hand. He then presses application: reconciliation is so urgent it interrupts worship (leave the gift at the altar and go be reconciled first) and so urgent it should be settled "quickly," lest judgment fall.

**Key terms (from commentary).** *Angry with his brother* — sinful anger "without cause": without just provocation, good end, or moderation; incipient murder in God's account. *Raca* — a scornful word from pride, disdaining a brother as empty. *You fool* — a spiteful word from hatred, condemning him as vile. *The three tribunals* (judgment, Sanhedrin, hell-fire) — the commentary reads all three as degrees of *divine* retribution, expressed through Jewish courts, not merely temporal penalties.

**Doctrinal anchors.** Treated as inspired and Scripture-interpreting-Scripture, this text shows (WLC Q99, via 5.21–22) that the law is spiritual and perfect, reaching understanding, will, and affections, forbidding the least degree of every sin. WLC Q136 (via 5.22) names sinful anger, hatred, and provoking words among sins forbidden in the sixth commandment; WLC Q135 (via 5.24) names readiness to be reconciled and forgiving of injuries among its required duties. WLC Q151 (via 5.22) supplies only its reading that words and actions aggravate sin conceived in the heart.

**Cross-references.** MRK.11.25 (forgive when you pray, via 5.23); 1JN.3.14–15 (hating a brother is murder, via 5.22); 1JN.4.20–21 (cannot love God while hating brother); 1JN.2.9 (hatred is walking in darkness); EPH.4.26–27 (be angry yet sin not); EPH.4.31–32 (put away wrath, be kind); JAS.3.6 (the tongue set on fire by hell); DEU.5.17 / EXO.20.13 / NUM.35.30–34 (the sixth commandment and blood-guilt); LUK.12.58–59 (agree with adversary on the way, via 5.25); PRO.25.8 (do not rush to strife); JAS.5.16 (confess and pray for one another); HEB.3.13 (exhort daily); LEV.6.2–6 (restitution before offering).

**Reading moves.** D7 = trace the "but I tell you" contrast (traditional narrowing → true spiritual extent) and the "therefore/first... then" of the altar case; note the shift at v25–26 where the courtroom picture, per the commentary, points beyond human quarrel to reconciliation with God — hold that as the commentary's careful reading, not a separate treatise.
