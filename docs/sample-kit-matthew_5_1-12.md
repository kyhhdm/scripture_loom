# Sample Session Kit — Matthew 5:1–12 (The Beatitudes)

A worked example of the four-page printable kit specified in `design-kit_generator.md`.

Assumed context: the family from the founding documents — **Aquila** (father, leader), **Priscilla** (mother), **Grace** (~10), **Liberty** (~7). Previous session: Matthew 4:1–11 (the temptation). Scripture text uses the World English Bible (public domain); the app would print the family's chosen translation.

Bracketed tags like `[D5]` show which fluency dimension each item is drawn from — they illustrate the library indexing and would **not** appear on the printed family pages (they do appear, small, on the leader guide).

## PAGE 1 — Leader Guide

*(for Aquila; used in preparation, kept beside him during the gathering)*

### Before you plan anything

> You are about to lead worship, not deliver a lesson. God will speak to your family in his Word — your preparation serves that, it cannot produce it.
>
> **First:** read Matthew 5:1–12 once, slowly, as a hearer — not yet as a teacher. What does it say to *you* today?
>
> **Then pray.** For yourself: a heart that believes what it reads. For each member by name: that God would open Grace's, Liberty's, and Priscilla's ears to his voice.

Only then, review the plan below.

---

**Passage:** Matthew 5:1–12 — Jesus begins the Sermon on the Mount

**Main aim:** Become familiar with the structure and main ideas of the Beatitudes — especially the repeated pattern *"Blessed are… for they…"*

**Session order:** opening prayer → opening recall → first reading (cold) → silent observation → hand out quests → second reading → narration → question round → vocabulary → connection round → memory verse → exit cards → closing prayer

### Opening recall — from Matthew 4:1–11 `[D4, D2]`

- Who was tempted in the wilderness, and by whom?
- How did Jesus answer each temptation? *(looking for: "with Scripture / 'It is written'")*
- What happened right after the temptation ended? `[D2]`

> Last session, Grace asked: *"Why didn't Jesus just tell the devil to leave at the beginning?"* — return to it together if there is a natural moment. `[D6, personalized line]`

### Listen for (this session's observation targets)

1. Can **Liberty** retell who came to Jesus and what he did, in order? `[D2 Event Sequence]`
2. Can **Grace** explain "blessed" in her own words? `[D3 Vocabulary]`
3. Does **either child** connect Jesus going up the mountain to teach, or any Beatitude, to something studied earlier? `[D5 Connections]` *(unprompted connection = mark ★)*

Watch for these three only. Everything else is a bonus.

### Key questions (after the second reading)

1. What words or phrases repeat? How many Beatitudes did you count? `[D3]`
2. Who does Jesus call blessed? Are these the people the world calls blessed? `[D7 Interpretation]`
3. Which saying is hardest to understand? `[D6 — collect on question cards]`
4. Which Beatitude can you remember without looking? `[D4]`

### Roles this session

| Role | Member |
|---|---|
| Reader (second reading) | Grace |
| Question keeper | Liberty |
| Word finder | Priscilla |
| Memory keeper | Liberty |
| Recorder (notebook page) | Priscilla |

### Memory verse

> **Matthew 5:9 — "Blessed are the peacemakers, for they shall be called children of God."**

### Closing

Exit cards, then pray — let the children's prayer topics come from their application cards if they offer them.

## PAGE 3 — Recall Activity: Match the Blessing

*(after narration; Liberty and Grace can work as a team, then check against the printed passage — the text is the answer key)* `[D4 Memory, D3 Vocabulary]`

Draw a line from each beginning to its ending. Careful — the endings are shuffled!

```text
Blessed are the poor in spirit …          … for they shall be comforted.

Blessed are those who mourn …             … for they shall inherit the earth.

Blessed are the gentle (meek) …           … for theirs is the Kingdom of Heaven.

Blessed are those who hunger and
thirst after righteousness …              … for they shall obtain mercy.

Blessed are the merciful …                … for they shall see God.

Blessed are the pure in heart …           … for they shall be filled.

Blessed are the peacemakers …             … for theirs is the Kingdom of Heaven.

Blessed are those who have been
persecuted for righteousness' sake …      … for they shall be called
                                            children of God.
```

**Bonus** `[D2]`: Two of the endings are the same. Which two beginnings share it? Circle them. *(This is how Jesus marks the beginning and end of the list — like bookends.)*

## Design notes on this sample

*(not printed; for the design record)*

- **Heart preparation is physically first** on page 1, before the plan — per the governing conviction. The leader cannot reach the logistics without passing the prayer.
- **Read-twice** is built into the session order: cold read → silent observation → quests → second read. The quest slips are the activation instrument.
- **Activation stages differ visibly**: Liberty gets a full quest (stage 1); Grace gets a category and writes her own question (stage 2). The `°` notation on the grid feeds the `prompted: no` flag that will eventually fade Grace's scaffold entirely.
- **Only three observation targets**, repeated on a reminder strip — the design fights the leader's temptation to observe everything.
- **The recall activity is auto-assemblable** from `key_facts` for this pericope (the eight blessing/promise pairs), including the bookend structure bonus — no bespoke authoring needed beyond review.
- **The personalized line** (Grace's question from Matthew 4) is templated composition from the member record, not generated text.
- **Theological guardrail check**: content treats the text as trustworthy throughout (the printed passage is the answer key); "blessed" is approached through the text's own pattern rather than speculative definitions; interpretation questions send readers back into the passage. Nothing hedges on what WCF ch. 1 affirms.
- Total: four pages plus quest slips — printable, and degradable to hand-copying (the quests and the grid fit on index cards).
