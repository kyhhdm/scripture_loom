# MAT-009 — The Temptation of Jesus (Matthew 4:1-11)

*20 items · brief: `content_bank/author/briefs/mat-009.md`*

## Theological brief (the base)

> # Theological Brief — MAT-009: The Temptation of Jesus (Matthew 4:1-11)
> 
> **Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Led by the Spirit into the wilderness, hungry after forty days' fasting, Jesus meets three escalating temptations (bread from stones, a presumptuous leap from the temple, worship of Satan for the kingdoms of the world). He answers each by citing Deuteronomy: "It is written." Defeated, the devil departs, and angels minister to Christ. The stress is his obedient, Scripture-armed victory over the tempter — not a treatise on fasting, angels, or worship.
> 
> **Key terms (from commentary).** "Tempted" — a real trial, not sin unless yielded to (MHC). "It is written" — though the eternal Word able to speak on his own authority, Christ chose to honor Scripture and appeal to what is written, as an example to us (MHC, v.4). "Tempt the Lord your God" — presuming on a promise rather than trusting it (JFB, v.7). "Worship... serve Him only" — exclusive allegiance (JFB, v.10).
> 
> **Doctrinal anchors.** Method: Christ models WCF 1 — Scripture is sufficient and self-interpreting; to Satan's misapplied promise (Ps 91) he answers with a governing precept from elsewhere (Deu 6:16), Scripture interpreting Scripture (WCF 1.9). Doctrine: this conflict belongs to Christ's humiliation in life (WLC Q48); the third temptation affirms exclusive worship of God alone (WCF 21.1-2; WLC Q104), and "tempting God" is a sin against the first commandment (WLC Q105).
> 
> **Cross-references.**
> - DEU.8.3 — source of "man shall not live by bread alone"
> - DEU.6.16 — source of "do not put the Lord to the test"
> - DEU.6.13-14 — source of "worship... serve Him only"
> - PSA.91.11-12 — the promise Satan misapplies
> - HEB.2.18 — Christ's own temptation fits him to help the tempted
> - EPH.6.17 — the word of God as the Spirit's sword
> 
> *Off-agenda: the sixth commandment (WLC Q135) and prayer (WLC Q179) proof-texts are noted only for phrases they ground, not this passage's subject.*

# MAT-014 — The Beatitudes (Matthew 5:3-12)

*18 items · brief: `content_bank/author/briefs/mat-014.md`*

## Theological brief (the base)

> # Theological Brief — MAT-014: The Beatitudes (Matthew 5:3-12)
> 
> **Passage's own emphasis (primary).** Jesus opens His sermon pronouncing kingdom blessing on Spirit-wrought characters — poor in spirit, mourning, meek, hungering for righteousness, merciful, pure in heart, peacemakers, persecuted — each "Blessed are..." joined to a fitting promise. The sequence runs from inward emptiness before God to outward suffering for righteousness, and vv.11-12 turn from "they" to direct address, "Blessed are you," binding the disciples' reproach to Christ's own name and to the prophets before them.
> 
> **Key terms (from commentary).** "Poor in spirit" — humble, needy dependence on God, not cowardice. "Mourn" — gracious, penitential sorrow, not despair. "Meek" — quietly submitted, gentle, slow to resent. "Hunger and thirst for righteousness" — earnest, repeated desire for it. "Pure in heart" — inward, not ceremonial, holiness. "Persecuted... for My sake" — Christ identifies His own cause with righteousness itself.
> 
> **Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): received as Christ's own inspired, sufficient words, each promise read alongside other Scripture. Doctrine: WCF 19.6 (cited on v.5, the meek inheriting the earth) reads such promises as the law's promises that "show God's approbation of obedience, and what blessings they may expect... though not as due to them by the law as a covenant of works" — blessing and reward, not merit — a principle that likewise bears on "great is your reward" (v.12). WLC Q172 is cited only for its reading of "poor in spirit"/"mourn" as the humble, needy, self-distrusting heart seeking grace outside itself.
> 
> **Cross-references.** ISA.66.2, PRO.29.23, PSA.34.18, PRO.16.19, LUK.18.14 — humble/contrite heart God regards (v.3). PSA.107.9, JHN.4.14, JHN.6.27 — desire satisfied/filled (v.6). PSA.37.11 — meek inherit the land (v.5). PSA.24.4, PSA.51.10 — clean/pure heart (v.8). HEB.12.14, JAS.3.16-18 — pursuing peace (v.9). JAS.1.12 — reward for enduring trial (v.10). REV.21.4 — mourning ended, comfort completed (v.4).

> Off-agenda note: WLC Q172's subject is admission to the Lord's Supper for a doubting believer; only its description of the poor-in-spirit/mourning heart is used here, and its sacramental topic is set aside as not this passage's concern.

# MAT-015 — Salt and Light (Matthew 5:13-16)

*16 items · brief: `content_bank/author/briefs/mat-015.md`*

## Theological brief (the base)

> # Theological Brief — MAT-015: Salt and Light (Matthew 5:13-16)
> 
> **Passage's own emphasis.** Jesus tells his disciples what they already ARE: "the salt of the earth" (v.13) and "the light of the world" (v.14). Salt carries a warning — losing its savor, it is good for nothing, thrown out and trampled (v.13); light is stated as inescapably visible — a city on a hill, a lamp set on a stand, not hidden (vv.14-15). Verse 16 gives the purpose: "let your light shine... that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven" — visible good works aim at God's glory, not the disciples' own.
> 
> **Key terms.** "Salt" — preserving influence against corruption; losing savor means becoming insipid, by nature irrecoverable (MHC). "Light" — Christ's own title, given to disciples only as they reflect his light (JFB); a city on a hill or lamp on a stand is inherently visible, not concealed. "Good deeds/works" — visible fruit prompting observers to glorify God, not the disciples.
> 
> **Doctrinal anchors.** Method (WCF 1): the text is Christ's own inspired, sufficient instruction. Doctrine (WCF 16.2, via v.16): good works "are the fruits and evidences of a true and lively faith," by which believers "manifest their thankfulness... and glorify God, whose workmanship they are" — fruit/evidence, not the ground of standing, matching v.16's purpose clause.
> 
> **Cross-references.** 1PE.2.12 (conduct seen, God glorified); COL.4.6 (salted speech); LUK.14.34-35 (salt losing savor); TIT.2.7 (works as model); PHP.2.15 (shine as lights); MRK.9.49-50 (salt in yourselves); JHN.15.8 (fruit glorifies the Father); JHN.8.12 (Christ as light); EPH.5.8-14 (children of light); 1JN.1.5-7 (walking in light); 1TH.5.5 (sons of light); LEV.2.13 (salt in offerings); HEB.6.4-6 (apostasy irrecoverable, like salt that cannot be re-salted); PHP.2.15-16 (shining amid a crooked generation).
> 
> Off-agenda: WCF 16.2's clauses on assurance and edification are set aside; v.16 grounds only the "glorify God" phrase.
