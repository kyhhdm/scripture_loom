# Scripture Loom — Core Principles and Methods

This document distills the working philosophy of the project from the three founding documents:

- `motto.md` — the Bible Fluency Method (the pedagogy)
- `unplug_assitant.md` — the invisible-assistant product design (the product shape)
- `feature-listening_transcription.md` — audio evidence capture (**optional, low priority**: high cost, and the unplugged paper workflow is preferred and must stand on its own)

Where the founding documents and this summary disagree, this document reflects the current decisions.

---

## The Governing Conviction: Worship, Not Academy

Scripture reading is not purely objective skill training. It is the revelation of the invisible God to our hearts: **God speaks to us in his Word.** This spiritual dimension is what distinguishes family worship from academic exercise, and it governs everything below.

The division of labor is strict:

- **The app trains fluency** — the natural means: familiarity, memory, attention, active reading.
- **Only the Spirit gives revelation** — the supernatural end the means serve.
- **The leader stands where the two meet** — and therefore the leader's faith is the one critical factor that cannot be automated, scaffolded, or measured.

Two consequences run through the whole design:

1. **The leader's heart is prepared, not just their materials.** The prepare phase addresses the leader's faith — prayer, reading the passage devotionally before preparing to teach it, the reminder that they are leading worship, not a class.
2. **Content is not theologically agnostic.** A fixed confessional standard — the Westminster Confession, especially Chapter 1 on Holy Scripture (its inspiration, infallibility, inerrancy, sufficiency, and clarity) — is the guardrail and lamppost for all content authoring and review. Note that WCF 1.9, "the infallible rule of interpretation of Scripture is Scripture itself," is the Connections dimension stated confessionally: the guardrail and the pedagogy agree.

The fluency methods below are means of grace-adjacent discipline, never a substitute for the encounter they prepare for. This is also the deepest reading of principle 4: the app records observable fluency and never measures faith, because that domain belongs to God and to the leader's discernment, not to software.

## Core Principles

### 1. Fluency before analysis

The goal is familiarity with Scripture's language, stories, people, and patterns. Theology and interpretation grow out of that foundation, not the reverse.

The learning order is:

> Hear → Remember → Recognize → Understand → Delight → Live

The Bible itself is the textbook; commentaries and teachers are helpers, not the center.

### 2. The app prepares the table, then leaves the room

> **Prepare digitally. Gather physically. Reflect intelligently.**

Computing belongs before and after the gathering, never during it. Any during-session feature — live AI questions, live scoring, live transcripts, dashboards, notifications, gamification, per-participant screens — is presumptively wrong.

### 3. Paper is the primary evidence medium, not a fallback

Children's written cards, notebook pages, drawings, and the leader's shorthand marks are first-class data. A handwritten question card is already structured, attributable, and preservable — better evidence than a transcript.

Photography bridges paper back into the app after the session. Audio, if ever used, is a passive backup memory at most.

### 4. Evidence, never judgment

Records state observable behavior:

- "Recalled three Beatitudes with hints."
- "Could not remember which Old Testament book Jesus quoted."
- "Connected 'peacemakers' with resolving a disagreement at school."

Never character or spiritual conclusions ("lacks faith", "spiritually mature", "was uninterested"). The app proposes; the leader confirms; only confirmed evidence becomes permanent.

### 5. The durable asset is the longitudinal per-member fluency record

Individual sessions, photos, and any recordings are disposable inputs. The product is the growing picture of each member's Bible fluency over time: passages studied, questions asked, recall demonstrated, connections made, vocabulary needing review.

### 6. The leader is a coach, not a lecturer or a clerk

The app amplifies the coaching role — good questions, observation targets, review scheduling — and must never impose data-entry work during the gathering. During a session the leader makes marks of a few characters at most; the post-session reflect flow takes about five minutes, not twenty.

### 7. Active reading over passive consumption — the scaffold is designed to fade

Most readers today are trained by social media into **scroll-mode**: continuous intake, no question held, no retention expected. A beginner brings scroll-mode to Scripture and finishes a passage with almost nothing. Fluency is impossible in passive mode, so the product's toolkit — pre-reading quests, questions, memory work, connection-finding, narration — is first of all **activation infrastructure**: it puts a question in the reader's mind so that reading becomes search, an exploration with its own motivation.

The scaffold exists to make itself unnecessary. At first the kit supplies the questions; the goal is that over months the reader supplies their own. The clearest signal of success is the **unprompted question** — a member asking without being prompted is worth more than any correct answer. The app fades each member's scaffolding as their unprompted questions appear.

This also gives the unplug principle a third, non-negotiable justification: the screen is the passive-mode context itself. You cannot train someone out of scroll-mode inside the medium that trained them into it. Paper, pencil, and a held question are the active-mode posture.

## Core Methods

### The three-phase cycle

This loop **is** the product:

```text
Prepare (digital)
    ↓
Gather (paper, unplugged)
    ↓
Reflect (digital, ≤5 minutes)
    ↓
feeds the next Prepare
```

- **Prepare** — begins with the leader's own heart: a prayer prompt and a devotional first reading of the passage before any planning. Then the leader selects the passage and the app assembles a main objective, review questions, discussion prompts, 2–3 observation targets, and a printable paper session kit.
- **Gather** — printed Scripture, Bibles, pencils, index cards, the family notebook. No screens.
- **Reflect** — the leader photographs paper artifacts, expands shorthand marks into confirmed observations, and the app updates each member's record and suggests the next session.

### Pre-reading quests and reading twice

Before the passage is read aloud, each member receives a small **quest** to hold in mind — a look-for, a prediction, a count, a character to track ("Listen for a phrase that repeats. How many times do you hear it?"). The question converts listening into search.

A session may read the passage twice: once cold with only "write one thing you noticed," then again with quests in hand. Members feel directly how much more the text yields when approached with a question — the habit-formation moment.

As a member's own questions begin appearing unprompted, their quests fade: full quest → category only ("find something that repeats") → "write your own quest" → no prompt needed (see principle 7).

### Designed evidence moments

The session structure itself produces evidence — no machine listening required. Each session intentionally includes moments that guarantee an artifact from every member, including the quiet child:

- **Opening recall** — each person answers one question from the previous session
- **Silent observation** — everyone writes one thing they notice
- **Question round** — each person contributes one question (or "I do not have one yet")
- **Retelling** — one child retells the passage while another checks the sequence
- **Connection round** — each person identifies one link to earlier passages
- **Exit card** — "One thing I learned / one thing I still wonder"

This is the load-bearing insight of the product: don't observe learning spontaneously — design the liturgy so it captures itself.

### Narration as the core check

If a learner can retell the passage accurately in their own words, they are beginning to own the text. Narration comes before interpretation questions.

### Focused observation targets

The leader watches for only **2–3 evidence targets per session**, suggested by the app during preparation and rotated over time across the fluency dimensions:

- People and places
- Event sequence
- Vocabulary
- Passage recall
- Interpretation
- Connections
- Questions
- Application

Never "evaluate everyone on everything."

### Rotating physical roles

Each member has a meaningful role, rotated across sessions. Roles create participation without screens, and each role emits observable evidence:

| Role | Does | Evidence produced |
|---|---|---|
| Reader | Reads the passage aloud | Reading fluency |
| Question keeper | Collects questions on cards | The family's questions |
| Word finder | Writes unfamiliar words | Vocabulary gaps |
| Story mapper | Tracks people, places, events | Sequence and structure grasp |
| Connector | Looks for links to earlier passages | Cross-Scripture connections |
| Memory keeper | Chooses a verse to remember | Memory verse record |
| Recorder | Writes observations in the notebook | The session's notebook page |

### Shorthand marks, expanded afterward

During the session the leader writes marks like `G-Q`, `L-R△`, `G-C★` (member initial + evidence code + optional qualifier). During reflect, the app prompts the leader to expand each mark into one confirmed sentence.

Symbols: `✓` clear evidence · `△` partial or needed help · `?` needs follow-up · `★` especially important moment.

Codes: `Q` question · `A` answer attempt · `R` recall · `C` connection · `U` uncertainty · `P` personal application.

### The Family Bible Fluency Notebook

The visible, shared, physical record. Each session adds a spread: left page *what we studied* (date, passage, people, places, events, key words, memory verse), right page *what we discovered* (questions, surprises, connections, review needs, prayer and application). The app keeps the searchable mirror; the notebook keeps the family's story.

### Repetition and continuous reading

Read whole books over time rather than isolated passages. Revisit the same stories, themes, and vocabulary repeatedly. The app's prepare phase is where this scheduling intelligence lives: spaced review of what a member missed sessions ago, executed on paper by the family.

## MVP orientation

The first version needs only:

1. **Preparation assistant** — passage in; objective, review questions, prompts, observation targets, and paper activity out
2. **Printable session kit** — leader guide, participant observation sheet, recall activity, leader evidence grid
3. **Post-session capture** — photograph the pages, confirm a handful of observations
4. **Longitudinal member record** — confirmed evidence organized per member, with suggested follow-ups

Audio recording and transcription can be added later without changing this core workflow — and the workflow must be fully useful without them.
